Prague

Return to Eastern Europe

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Apr 12, 2016

My only other really Eastern European experience so far was in Budapest, which I loved. Yes, Vienna is further east than Prague, but culturally it was far more western. Prague was actually laid out a lot like Budapest and had a similar cultural feeling, so if you couldn’t guess I really liked Prague as well. Before I get into that though, there were a couple of things to mention pre-Prague. There was a good deal of rain in the forecast for my weekend, so I was incredibly discouraged to realize that I lost my backpack cover, probably on a plane/train on my way home from Barcelona. Especially with my plans for another beach city next weekend, that thing was so convenient for keeping sand out of my bag and all of my things, not to mention the intended rain protection - RIP. My backpack is also falling apart. I guess that it wasn’t made to be carried all day 4+ days a week, RIPx2. Other dumb thing to mention before Prague – the first picture in the Prague folder is actually the waiting room in the Saarbruecken train station. I was there and thought to snap a picture of that frozen hell where I slept with the homeless guy that one time to share it with you.

So Prague. I took an overnight train there which was so great. I recently found a huge flaw in the train planning application that we have been using all semester which has been limiting our ability to take overnight trains more so I wish that we had started utilizing those sooner, but nonetheless this one was perf. We spread out so I had one of the glass door cage rooms with only one other guy in it, so I was able to lay down and stretch out over three of the most comfortable train seats you can get. It was honestly some of the best sleep that I have had in a long time. When I woke up I met the other guy in my box who was a college student from USC who was pretty cool, so that pretty cool. There seems to be a recurring theme of meeting other fun US college people on trains.

Most of the GTL undergrads are all in one giant 130 person groupme that we use for information, favors, and planning trips and whatever, but most travel groups make separate groupme groups for the sake of planning and keeping in touch with the people in the same place as them. So naturally we made a group which started out with the 8 or so people that I normally travel with, plus a couple of other random people. Overnight the randos multiplied though and we ended up with a group of 21 GTL students traveling together in Prague. Obviously during the days we were splintered into small groups for sightseeing and stuff, but we all came together for a couple of meals and nightlife entertainment so we had a huge group for those.

I did another free walking tour here since I have had pretty good luck with those lately. It only covered half of the city, but our tour guide was very cool and gave us lots of tips afterwards for places to go and local insight into some of the coolest bars and attractions that otherwise we absolutely would not have found. I did some aimless wandering with the group who was staying in the same hostel that I was for the rest of the day and then we got a taste of the Prague nightlife foretold to be legendary.

A quick aside about my hostel – the group that I normally travel with were looking into hostels and Airbnbs for the weekend and I came across a martial arts gym available to rent relatively cheaply with rubber weapons and training gear available to rent. I posted a link to it in our groupme jokingly, but some people really clung to the idea. I didn’t see it panning out as we needed a good number of people to get a good rate, so I went ahead and booked a hostel. Well jokes on me, they did end up getting enough people for the gym and it was awesome. They ended up breaking so much stuff, including a window within the first 5 minutes. I myself went to see it one night and accumulated some fines for breakages myself, but that is another story. The host was apparently really cool about all of it, as though it happens every weekend and had an itemized list of fines prepared which was pretty reasonably priced so it all worked out well enough.

Going back now to Prague nightlife – it is very well known for its pub crawls so we ended up doing one of those. The way that every single one of them works here is a period before the crawl (for us it was 2.5 hrs) of an open bar unlimited everything followed by a more traditional experience. I was very partial to a boat party that was the same setup but with a party on a boat instead of the actual bar crawling but the google reviews were far from stellar so we ended up playing it safe with what we knew was supposed to be good. I’m not going to go into much detail on this experience here, but the company’s actual slogan in “The best night that you will never remember” so you can ask me personally how that night went.

Alcohol is probably one of the biggest elements of the culture here, which I wasn’t really expecting. I went to Munich for instance fully expecting that, but in my opinion Prague has been the most drinking-centric city that I have visited. According to our tour guide it is the city with the most beer consumed per person of anywhere in the world, but tour guides also have a tendency to skew those types of rankings so don’t quote me on that. It also followed suit of Budapest with how incredibly cheap it was. Subway was the most expensive meal that I got here as compared to multiple nice meals of Czech food in sit down restaurants with beer(s) included if that gives you any idea of how cheap things were here – though Subway was weirdly overpriced as well. Since I have found myself on the subject of fast food in my ramblings I will throw it in here: McDonald’s count adds the Czech Republic bringing the total to 12 #cultured. The main McDonald’s here actually has some interesting backstory to it. The main huge square that was the site of the Velvet Revolution bringing an end to communism here was the site of the first McDonald’s in Prague – intentionally to serve as a symbol of capitalism and reminder of the communist oppression prior. Pretty weird symbol even for me, but the inside was super nice and modern so I can’t complain. Relating back to the alcohol centered culture here, there is nothing better for a killer hangover than 5 McCheeseburgers which I will attest to.

Saturday was my main sightseeing day. I woke up pretty late, as seems to be the trend increasingly as the semester has gone on, but then spent from 11am onward into the night walking pretty consistently. I’m going to have to get some fitbit data from someone that I was with because it was probably one of the days with the most walking that I have had on this trip. Highlight reel: a lot of cool buildings, a castle, some nice cityscapes, a couple of parks and the top of a tower modeled after the Eiffel Tower but on a mountain… and a third of the size. One cool thing that came from the recommendation of my tour guide was visiting the brewery inside of a monastery where the monks microbrew their own beers which have even won best beer in the world a couple of times. I promise that I haven’t become a raging alcoholic here, just most of the attractions of Prague seemed to involve drinking in some unique way so it is coming up a lot in this post.

Most people left on Sunday morning, which I considered given an upcoming test on Wed that I should study for, but staying for the day and leaving that night meant having time to visit the 4th best zoo in the world. Going back to how cheap everything is, 5 of us split 2 Ubers each way ~17ish minute drive and spent less than $5/ea roundtrip + the zoo was stupidly cheap too, especially considering its size and status. My only other zoo experience was in Atlanta so maybe my reference level isn’t the best, but this zoo was huge. It was really cool, had an incredibly diverse selection of animals. Unfortunately most things that would have been a bit nicer to see outdoors had to be inside during the cold months. There was an elephant born there 5 days prior so it was a bit crowded but got to see that too. We didn’t have a ton of time, so we only were able to spend a few hours there and didn’t see quite everything, but it was still a ton of stuff and a fun group to do it with.

After the zoo we went to The Pub, a restaurant with beer taps right in the center of your table. It keeps track of the exact amount that you each pour similar to a gas pump, and they have a projector showing the competition between each table for who has drank the most. It switches between the tables within the same location and the competition between the different branches (it’s a chain) across a few different countries. It had a touch screen on top of the tap that you could order all of your food from and everything too. It was like a ziosk on steroids. I feel like one of these would clean up in the US, especially in a college town where you could have different fraternities and stuff competing against each other.

We started our overnight journey back to Metz after that. The first train that we got on we stayed on for 11 hours and when we first boarded there were no open non-reservation seats. We found a floor in the bike room to sit/lay in, turned on some music on a portable speaker, and planned to have some fun with it. To be honest most of us were probably asleep within the first 10 minutes though, and it wasn’t even 7pm. After the first stop a lot of spaces opened up so we could actually have seats. For a couple of hours we had the cage sleeper rooms and I honestly had a couple of hours of the soundest sleep of my life, or at least in months, in there. They are so comfortable and something about trains makes me so tired already. Around 12 we had to move to a different section because the train cars split off to different places so we had to move to suckier seats. At this point I had already had 5.5 hours of sleep, close to a normal night for me, but it was so early still so it is safe to say that my sleep cycle is going to need some hardcore recovery this week.

Other random things: I got to wear shorts in France a couple of days last week and then again today which is great. Given how much I despise cold weather, it has taken far too long to warm up here so it is a nice change of pace. I think that is actually it. Weird to think that next week is my last week of normal classes before finals – those things are really sneaking up on us. Next weekend I am going to Marseille for some beaches and outdoor adventuring, and my last normal travel weekend I am going to London. After that it is just finals and one last weekend in Paris before my flight home. The semester started off moving so slowly when everything was so new (not that that is a bad thing!) but now lately time is just flying by.

I noticed that I had the option to go inside so I did and was able to walk around the place including multiple staircases and seating areas, and with how high quality the pictures were could even read the wall menus and posters. It was mind blowing. Needless to say when I stumbled upon the same location in my travels this weekend I went inside and it happens to be one of the nicest views in all of Prague from that Starbucks Coffee, go figure.
