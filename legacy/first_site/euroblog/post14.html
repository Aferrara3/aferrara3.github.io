Short Weekend Grab Bag

Paintball, Amsterdam, and Random Stuff

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Apr 4, 2016

Given that we had a long weekend for Easter last week, those Monday classes that we missed were shifted to Friday this week resulting in a short weekend. Actually it is probably more aptly described as a normal length weekend, but we at GTL have grown accustomed to our 3.5 day breaks each week. Fortunately for me, one of my three classes was cancelled and one was skippable, so other than the usual hell that is having to wake up before 8am for class, my day was free to attend the paintball event that GTL BDE was hosting. BDE stands for Bureau des Etudiants which is just like SGA except they use their funding for fun events instead of progressive things which is way better IMO.

I had never played paintball before, so it was another, albeit a bit more unexpected, new experience while traveling abroad. The fields were huge and had a ton of variety of structures and huge abandoned buildings and stuff. I don’t know how it compares to fields in the US, but it was pretty damn cool. In the US I would have expected tons of safety information and probably some staff watching you the whole time, but here we were just handed guns and told to have fun with free range over the whole property. Being here in multiple situations as well as talking to French students has shed some light on how over-the-top and self-entitled the US culture is towards safety and really can be a shame for some things.

Given the short weekend, everyone was incredibly indecisive about where they were going, myself included. It didn’t seem worth it to spend half of the weekend on trains to have a single day in a city and come right back home, so a group of friends and I were planning on visiting Normandy. Northern coast of France, probably pretty touristy, must be easy to get to, right? Wrong. That shit is a logistical nightmare. We had planned out a way to make it work with (reserved) trains though Paris and renting a car to get around there, but it was all so rushed and sensitive to unforeseen time issues that we ended up not going through with it. I still would like to get there, and still may when in Paris for a few days before flying home, but won’t be heartbroken if I don’t make it. I’m glad that I went when I was younger and still remember it as that definitely takes some of the pressure off of getting there for me.

I was then planning on going to Luxembourg and maybe another day trip somewhere else on Sunday, but also didn’t like that option because I could go to any of those close places after class one day during the week so it felt wasteful of a weekend. James then concocted this disgusting plan to go to Amsterdam without getting a hostel. We decided to see Amsterdam during the day, and then just enjoy the nightlife until the trains started running again in the morning. It doesn’t really sound like that bad of an idea, until you try to go 24 hours of activity after a train-quality nights sleep.

We took overnight trains and got to Amsterdam around 9am. We met up with Diane who was coming from Germany to join us on our 24 hour marathon day and spent most of the day seeing the city. The first time that I went earlier in the semester we were all really bad at traveling. We spread ourselves too thin with places and were just bad at seeing things. It is kind of hard to put words to, but there is some skill to travelling properly the way that we are and we just didn’t have it yet. This time we were a lot more successful in seeing a lot of the sights. We jumped on a free walking tour with a pretty knowledgeable guide. I’m not sure how she stacked up to a couple of the other awesome free walking tour guides that I have had, but she was leagues better than the slow-moving trainwreck that my tour guide was the last time that I was here. Other than that we spent a good bit of time hanging out at a park and just having a really chill day.

We didn’t really see as much of the nightlife as we might have liked to this time around. I think that the public-transit sleep and day of walking around caught up to us which took some of the wind out of the “bars until sunrise” sails. It takes a lot of energy to keep up with a party atmosphere all night and this time we just didn’t have it, so at some point ended up sleeping at the train station and in a McDonalds waiting on the morning trains and buses to start running. It really wasn’t helping how much colder than expected it was or the fact that it was lightly raining all evening/night. All in all it was definitely still a really fun weekend, but the night time experience definitely was less than ideal. In hindsight it may have been worth booking a 40 euro/night hostel to have a bed for the night or at least nap in the evening to be ready to stay out all night. James and I were planning on stopping in Luxembourg on our way home and seeing that during the day on Sunday, but with how tired we were I highly doubt that we would have made it off of the train had we even picked a better route that went through there.

Lessons learned, or rather reiterated, were that things run more smoothly with even a little bit of forward planning and that beds are totally worth it.

Back in Metz

That was all that I had about this weekend, but there is more in my life than weekend travel sometimes. Last week I had Monday off and then missed (some not-so-important) classes on Tuesday flying back from Barcelona. On Thursday and Friday I only had one class (or missed class bc paintball) so really I only had 1 full day of classes last week. If you add together the other two short days of classes let’s call it 2 days worth of class last week total. Granted those 2 days were full of tests, long homework assignments, and a project/presentation so they were some of the busiest days that I have had at GTL, but still it gives school this real feeling of being a footnote to my traveling – not that that is a bad thing just interesting.

After having a lot of stuff due last week, this week I have absolutely nothing. It’s weird, no tests, no presentations, and no weekly homework for an unknown reason. Some of my classes are even cancelled because my professors teach part time at GTL and part time at French Universities and the French schools are on spring break this week. It’s almost concerning how empty my calendar is for this week. I will do some contract work and housekeeping stuff, but I forsee a good bit of Netflix and catching up on sleep this week.

Speaking of Sleep

I have no room at all to complain about sleep here, but I still will. When I have been in Atlanta in recent semesters it has become all too common to stay up all night once or twice a week, and be up into the early AM hours a couple more. School and sleep there just don’t mix. This week was the first time that I came close to that kind of sleep schedule here. I slept in on Easter Sunday, but since there haven’t really had much sleep, even though I am mostly to blame. On Monday my only sleep was in an airport and on a plane which was followed by a night of working on a project, studying for a test, and prepping a presentation most of the night. The next night was then spent attempting to get all of my normal weekly homework that I had to do since professors don’t really care that it was a short week and my priorities are elsewhere for the weekends. Finally on Thursday night I could have slept some, but hey I was finished with everything so why not hang out with people and start a movie after midnight when you have class at 8am? Spent Friday night on trains not really sleeping and Saturday night in Amsterdam without a hostel. Sunday night again I could have slept but that lure of Netflix in bed is too strong. Take all of this and you have me falling asleep while typing this from my self-inflicted sleep deprivation. Just trying to make it feel more like I’m back in Atlanta, ya know?

I think that is actually everything that I have now.
