Paris

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Jan 19, 2016

Wow, Paris.

It only took one weekend there for Paris to easily skyrocket up there to one of my favorite cities, if not topping the list all together. You are constantly surrounded by the most intricate and incredible architecture throughout every street. The different quarters and districts each have such a unique feel of culture with their respective shops and sounds - absolutely wonderful first place to visit.

Our first stop after getting off of the train was Notre Dame Cathedral. It is a really interesting construction considering how completely different it looks from each side, in some cases almost unidentifiable at first to one another. Inside was gorgeous of course and had some interesting relics and pieces from the Church's history.

We followed up Notre Dame with a visit to the adjacent Latin Quarter for some small stops and lunch. The restaurants here, as well as later noted throughout Paris, operate differently from what I have at least experienced in the US. People stand outside each of the restaurants trying to convince you to choose theirs. It is almost overwhelming walking down the street with everyone shouting specials and prices to you, but it creates a neat atmosphere. The food here was great and surprisingly reasonably priced.

You can't go to Paris and not go to the Louvre, so we tried to at least squeeze in the most notable exhibits before they closed. The Louvre Palace that the museum is built in(?)/under had the most impressively detailed stonework that I have seen yet. As with Notre Dame and many of the other notable structures here, it is hard to even imagine how they were constructed with such detail given the age in which they were built. We only had a couple of hours inside, and it is definitely large enough to spend an entire day inside without seeing it all, so I will have to go back and spend some more time there at the end of the semester when I am staying there for 5 additional days.

As an aside regarding the metro - it is the most efficient transportation I have ever experienced and way way easier to navigate than I had expected. Particularly on Sunday we found ourselves taking it often to get around with great ease, making it possible to see as much as we were able to in such a short time. Granted we had an unfortunate experience on the metro with stupid fines and regulations, but overall I absolutely wish that this type of transportation was better supported in the US. I realize that NY and other cities may have a good subway infrastructure, but my experience is with Atlanta and MARTA and that's kind of a joke.

We had a pretty authentic French dinner before going out at night, but the bar scene in Paris, at least where we were, was very different from what I have ever seen in America or even Metz for that matter. All of the bars were completely deserted, or packed to the point where you couldn't even stand inside. All of the bars were extremely cramped and filled with tables without any real open spaces - definitely didn't seem like a particularly social environment as compared to in the US or Metz.

Our Airbnb the first night was baller. We had a flat in downtown Paris all to ourselves as the host family was out of town. Split that 7 ways and you are paying pocket change for prime location. We were located right next to Saint-Jean-Baptiste de Belleville - another gorgeous Church where I attended mass on Sunday. Sitting in a French mass was also a really unique and interesting experience.

I had crepes for brunch on Sunday underneath the Eiffel Tower, so yeah, that was nice. Crepes here - also baller, those should be more of a thing in America. We went up as high as we could, though the top was closed for renovations but we were able to climb the 669 up to the second level. The view of Paris from here was awesome, and it even started snowing while we were up there. We followed up the Eiffel Tower by walking to the Champs-Elysees and down the mile long stretch lined with stores on both sides to the Arc de Triomphe - gotta hit all of the major icons.

Next major stop was Sacre-Coeur Basilica, which I think may have stolen the show as far as Churches seen so far goes. It was on Sunday so there was service going on inside as well which was a nice touch for the atmosphere there. Behind the basilica there is an art square with tons of amazing paintings and portraits - while no art buff even I was tempted to get something if I had any way to easily transport it back. Dinner in Paris again so obviously had to get... Italian food? The people there seemed too friendly not to and the food was incredible so it was worth going against the authentic Paris experience for Italian. You might have noticed that I focus a lot on food, and that is because the food here is better. America you have been called out.

Ended the stay seeing the Eiffel Tower again but lit up at night. You should definitely go look at my pics, I got some very nice ones of this. I am very glad that I ended up getting this new phone with an awesome camera on it and didn't let anyone convince me otherwise, (you know who you are) so shoutout to Samsung for that one. Also the battery life on it since I am too forgetful to ever have that gps logger on me for maps allowing me to have gps on at all times and then just pull my location history from google is clutch, because I still go the Tech and like the data and maps and stuff.

That's about all that I have and after 1000 words I'm feeling pretty done typing, so enjoy!

Oh, also I make occasional notes on my phone of things that I want to include, and somehow it slipped through the cracks thus far, but the croissants here kick ass. The knock off kind that you get in the US don't even compare, and I already really liked those.
