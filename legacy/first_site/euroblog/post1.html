First Post

Flights and moving in and all that good stuff

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Jan 11, 2016

Sup fam. Enjoy the astronaut banner, because I am way too lazy right now to find a more reasonable pic.

First some fun facts about my plane: It was surprisingly nice and roomy. In seat USB charging and AC outlets was pretty sweet. The touch screen entertainment systems in the back of the seat in front of you had tons of new movies and games + flight maps, interesting flight info, and a feed from a camera mounted under the plane. Everything is seeming pretty sweet, and then Andre the Giant came and sat down next to me and a baby wouldn't stop crying. Both of those issues pretty much resolved themselves though as Andre moved to a new seat leaving me with plenty of extra space and the baby had stopped by the end of the first movie that I watched. Turbulence still makes me really anxious though, so on the flight home I may need a few more of those complimentary Heinekens. My map doesn't reflect the flight, because my gps logger apparently had issues maintaining a lock at 650 mph. -- One last note about the plane ride, to put the size of European cities in comparison, even the smaller US towns flown over such as Greensboro looked so much more expansive and lit up from the sky than everything seen at night over Europe short of Paris.

Once we got to the airport we found our way to the shuttle meet up and waited for a couple of hours for delayed flights. During this time I ate my inaugural meal in France - a Big Mac. The combo did come with a beer though because apparently they are way more frat over here. Once everyone got there we started the bus ride to Metz. Now I was under the impression that it was like an hour by train and 2 by bus, but apparently it is more like an hour by train and 4 by bus, so that was unfortunate. We got here though and the rooms are pretty dank - nice to have my own bedroom and bathroom again. We had some free pizza social thing, and then I walked to the local Cora with some guys to go pick up beer. I left thinking it would be pretty close, but I failed to realize that I was signing up for a >1mi trek each way carrying supplies on the way back. It was chill though, pretty cold and raining a bit but the store was insane so it was worth it. It is a lot like a super WalMart on steroids, with a bonus liquor store bigger than any standalone one that I have ever been to. Since then I have just been unpacking and stuff. I would like to go to bed soon, and had intended to power through the day without sleeping but the 4 hour bus nap ruined my plans for fixing my sleep schedule on day 1. Anyways, I'm really bored of writing this now so enjoy the update.
