Barcelona

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Mar 31, 2016

Not Barcelona

If you see that heading and think “Hey this post was titled Barcelona and that is all that I came for”, skip over the next couple of sections – a few housekeeping things to talk about first.

Belgium

Let’s talk a little bit about Belgium and the state of Europe this past week. I am sure that you are all aware of the attacks that occurred in Brussels last Tuesday, as well as across Europe lately in Turkey and the Ivory Coast. Brussels was definitely the worst so far for most GTL students because it was definitely the closest to home, so there were a couple of days of a constant buzz about the state of affairs around us and how it may affect travel plans. There is a really nicely written peace about the situation on the GTL official blog here. For me it was a little extra eerie, as I had already booked my flight back from Barcelona into the Brussels Zaventem airport that was attacked for the following Tuesday morning. Granted one week (and 30 minutes) is a pretty big difference, but still the thought that which Tuesday morning someone picked on a calendar was the difference between being there or not is still somewhat unsettling. As multiple people have asked – no, this doesn’t change anything about any travel plans that I have. Can’t let fear of the highly unlikely paralyze you, and I still have lots to see here.

There was a noticeable increase in security though, particularly in Paris. When I first stepped off of the train at the main station I walked past about 25 well-armed-not-your-average-beat-cop type security on my short walk to the nearest exit.

Pre-Barcelona/Paris

This week I was going to go to London, but those plans slowly fell apart. Honestly it made a lot more sense for a few reasons to go a few weeks later anyways, but I had already booked a Megabus and didn’t want to part with that sweet, sweet 3 dollars to move my reservation. Most of my friends that I normally travel with had already booked the trip weeks in advance for some reason, so my last minute change did mean having to take different trains and stay in a different hostel, but those ended up turing into pretty positive things. Booking so late for a big travel weekend did mean some hardcore price gouging though on train reservations, so that was lame.

Getting my train reservations was actually a huge pain in the ass. Apparently a lot of people travel Easter weekend - who knew? Every train that I wanted to take was completely booked for Eurail pass holders, so I made like 10 contingency plans looking at making my own routes through random cities in the right direction. The Metz train station is the worst because apparently they speak French in France, another surprise, and the people at our train station don’t take to the most kindly to dealing with non-French speakers. Tbh that’s a total understatement – the staff at our train station is comprised mostly of complete asshats, and with the very friendly and English proficient staff at the Luxembourg station only 45 minutes away by train I opted to deal with them. All of my great convoluted train plans also failed, but the staff there is awesome and helped me find a route that at least put me in Barcelona on Friday. I still haven’t visited Luxembourg yet even though it is so close; actually I had never even left the train station until this weekend when I took a couple of steps outside and then turned around and walked right back in, so that kinda counts.

The trains that I ended up on gave me a 4 hour connection in Paris, which for some reason I deemed not enough time to actually do anything. If you aren’t familiar with it, Paris has a kickass metro that can get you anywhere in like 15 minutes so I absolutely could have done something cool with this time instead of chilling at a Subway for an hour – their new fajita sub is fire though if you have that back in the US, but I digress. I ended up on the train sitting with a college student from California and his dad. They were super friendly, had already been to Barcelona so had solid tips for seeing the city, and obviously spoke English which alone is a nice advantage over who I could have been sitting with – definitely made the 6.5 hour train from Paris – Barcelona more pleasant.

BARCELONA

As soon as I got off of the train the first thing that I saw were signs advertising fast food deals for KFC and McDonald’s better than anything I have even seen in the US, so naturally I already loved Barcelona. The metro system was super clean and organized, so that was pretty chill too. My hostel was 12 stories and set up much more like a hotel than I have grown accustomed to lately. It was actually the first place that I have been that used key cards, which was refreshing to finally be staying somewhere in this century. I wandered around a bit at night on my way to meet up with all of my friends who had beat me here because I failed to plan and loved the city. There were some really unique buildings that were lit up and extremely colorful. There were some random colorful leds in the ground that were interactive and would change colors from sounds around them. It was just a lot of really random cool stuff that I liked.

It was my first night in Barcelona, so obviously we went on a pub crawl – though given the nightlife scene in Barcelona, closer to a club crawl ™. Saw some cool places, ran into some random American students including other GTL people. On that note, Barcelona was the place to be this weekend. I went with a group of 11 + we ran into 3 more GTLers on the pub crawl + 3 more GTLers in my hostel + 2 more at this park and beach + 5 other GT students interning/studying elsewhere in Europe = 24 GT students that we knew (of) who were there this weekend.

Random note, but McDonald’s here again had some unique aspects to it, but I’ve stopped my McDonald’s ramblings here. Expect another post just about that soon.

During the day on Saturday I did a lot of the sightseeing in the city. I saw the Cathedral, walked all of Las Ramblas, etc. There was a lot of English everywhere on signs, in stores, on products etc. Plus being able to read enough of the Spanish to get the jist of things was nice compared to most of the countries I have gone to. The Spanish seem to really like American sports which was really surprising. That or they are strictly targeting tourists with shops of all NFL/NBA/MLB apparel but I choose to believe the former. Spent some time at Barceloneta during the day as well, the main beach there. The weather all weekend wasn’t really warm enough to be beach weather, but warm enough to force it to be beach weather in the sun so it kind of worked out.

I think that it was this afternoon that I made the pilgrimage that I have been waiting my entire trip for. It was about 45 minutes by train/metro to get there, but absolutely worth it for… Taco Bell. In the US I eat a lot of Taco Bell. Back when I lived in Cumming where there wasn’t much to do it was where I would meet up with friends there to go do other stuff and built this brand loyalty to it. In Europe, and pretty much the rest of the world, Taco Bell has more or less failed miserably. Not in Spain though. There are 21 branches in Spain (supposedly though I could only find 1 of the alleged 2 in Barca) and this is probably the closest that I would be to one in Europe so I had to make the trip. The menu was different but equally perfect. There was no Baha Blast, but they served beer instead so it was forgiven. I said that I wouldn’t obsess over cultural differences observed between McDondald’s anymore, but never said anything about TBell.

I had another interesting first experience on Saturday night of my life – visiting a casino. I went in to it knowing how stacked against me the odds were and wasn’t stupid enough to blow a ton of money… only some money. I was up at one point too, but you never want to pull out on top and my slot machine was hot. I ended up cashing out with I think about 4 cents, so it didn’t go the best, but it was definitely fun and worth the entertainment cost.

This brings us to Easter Sunday. Honestly this year just didn’t have much of a holiday feel to it given how preoccupied I have been with traveling every weekend. I slept in for the first time in a long time, maybe even since I have been here. I walked the entire length of the beaches of Barcelona, including the nude section where the gender ratio was 10:1 with an average age around 90 – 0/10 would not recommend. Definitely need to review that place on trip advisor. The rest of the beaches were nice though and I met a friendly pit bull so it is hard to compete with that. Afterwards I went to Park Guell which was absolutely gorgeous. There is a peak there that offers a full 360 degree view of Barcelona and the surrounding area – just incredible, the pics don’t do it justice. Other than that I just chilled on the beach, had some Spanish food, pretty chill.

Monday I don’t know if I even left the beach. It was the warmest day so far and we had seen most of the sights so we took a more or less complete beach day. The water was way too cold to go in, but I am perfectly content sitting in the sand all day. One thing about the beaches here are the constant barrage of vendors. There are guys constantly trying to get you to buy “Mojitos” that are actually just lime juice and fraction of a shot of watered down rum. There are other guys selling these tapestry like sheet things to sit on. Add in henna tattoo people, hair braiders, soo many massage people, beer and water vendors, it was constant constant constant. To be fair, across our group we bought multiple of everything while there, but still it got quite annoying at times. It was kind of a progression from finding them neat, to annoying, to finding them amusing. Speaking of these vendors, Barcelona was absolutely the city that I have been the most solicited to buy drugs. Most of the beach vendors probably make most of their money this way, and especially at night around the bars and clubs I was constantly being offered an array of party-type drugs for purchase. Amsterdam gets the bad rap for it, but it is way more controlled and less pervasive there. Between all of these vendors I had some funny interactions though, but not worth typing them out here, just ask.

We stayed out pretty late Monday night and then slept in the airport for our early morning flight back on Tuesday morning. Ryainair diverted our flight on pretty short notice away from Zaventem as there have been delays in getting that back to full swing, so we ended up flying into a small airport in Belgium, but timing actually ended up working out really well from there for trains. I hated flying after Jamaica and my flight here, but was so pleasantly surprised by my flight experiences o discount airlines in Italy. This flight itself wasn’t that bad, or at least I slept through it, but the landing sucked and that was the only part that I was awake for so I’m back to not loving flying.

Now I have been back in Metz for what in total counts for like 2 days of classes and tomorrow will be spending the day playing some GT subsidized paintball and then leaving for wherever my next trip is to. It is Thursday night and I still don’t know where I am going so that isn’t great. I had planned to go to Normandy this weekend but that proved to be logisitically unnecessarily difficult so now idk, we’ll see where I end up. It is a shorter weekend since we have classes tomorrow (going to one before paintball) to make up for having Monday off, so I will probably stay close-by, maybe Luxembourg or Lyon.

Looking back I really didn’t take very many pictures this weekend. I spent the majority of my time probably at the beach, so you’ve seen those before and don’t need any pictures anyways.
