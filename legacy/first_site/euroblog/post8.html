Munich

And some other stuff

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Mar 2, 2016

This past weekend I went to Munich for Starkbierzeit (Strong Beer Festival). I don’t have a ton to say about the trip which is kind of good because all of my blog posts have been dominated by the play-by-plays of my trips and I do other stuff too sometimes.

Munich

I left early Friday morning for Munich. 5 of the 10 on our trip had planned on taking the pre-6am train out of Metz, but only 1 actually made it up that early and safe to say that it wasn’t going to be me. At the beginning of the trip getting up early all of the time was super easy – I feel like given the adjustment to the new time zone I had also adjusted in such a way that my mornings starting way earlier here wasn’t a big deal but that definitely doesn’t apply anymore. I spent Friday afternoon just exploring Munich a bit and seeing some of the touristy stuff. I would say that Munich is probably a better city to visit when the weather is warm. For instance The Englischer Gartens looked pretty cool on Google with tons of people just hanging out on the lawns everywhere drinking and tanning and whatever, and there wasn’t quite that same atmosphere when the ground was snow covered. We checked out a few of the beer halls that night, including dinner and drinks at Hofbrauhaus, supposedly the most famous beer hall in the world. It really did live up to a city famous for its beer – seeming to be a pretty significant part of the local culture from breweries on every other corner to some more unique ways. We ended our day with 5 of us stuck in the smallest glass elevator in the world at our airbnb for an hour and a half while a technician tried to get us out. The pic in the "other" folder of me with some random guy in the yellow vest is with the tech who got us out. It sucked, but was kinda funny.

Our plan for Saturday was really just focused on the Strong Beer Festival, but since that didn’t start until 10:30am we decided to make a quick visit to Deutsches Museum in the morning. Again I guess living under a rock, most of us didn’t realize that it was the largest science and technology museum in the world. As you can probably imagine, you drop a bunch of tech nerds in our Mecca and we aren’t going to make much of a “quick stop,” but rather end up like a bunch of kids in a candy store. I ended up staying there for like 4 hours and that was still the super-speed round in light of the festival. I could have spent days in there, hell I could have spent all 4 of those hours in the microelectronics area alone. Seriously that place is magical – like way more than Disneyworld even thinks that it is /nerd. If you want a kid to decide that they want to be a STEM student, all it takes is a $4 admission ticket… and a small trip to Munich.

After that we spent the rest of our day at the Paulaner Nockherberg Brewery, the heart of the Strong Beer Festival. It goes on for a few weeks and every beer hall does their own thing, but Fridays and Saturdays at Paulaner Nockherberg are where the party is supposed to be and it definitely did not let us down. It was cool seeing the progression of the day from 2:00 when everyone is sitting at their tables talking amongst themselves eating to the afternoon and evening where every single person is standing on the long benches and tables singing along with the band and drinking songs waving their liter steins in the air together. It was such a cool atmosphere and everything was delicious - though $11/beer puts a nice dent in a college student’s wallet, totally worth it for the experience. The night went pretty awry with some unfortunate circumstances and law enforcement, but I will not go into any detail on that here.

Sunday morning I went to BMW Welt, their showroom for all of their newest cars with some really unique and interactive displays. I didn’t have time to visit the BMW museum as well unfortunately, but the Welt was more than cool enough for me. I made one more stop at the Olympics Park there which was really modern looking with some gorgeous architecture, very surprising considering that the Olympics were held there in the early 70s. Munich overall actually shared this very modern look which is something that I appreciated a lot considering the more dated feeling of most of the European cities that I have visited. The only Olympic site that I have ever seen in person in the US is Atlanta, from the late 90s at that, and having now been to multiple Olympic sites in Europe they are sooo much more impressive – not even on the same scale.

Oh one other thing. I had originally planned on going to see Neuwachstein Castle on Sunday morning, but decided to wait since in 2.5 weeks my sister and Aunt will be in town(!) and plan on going there so I can just visit it with them.

Metz

School is going well. I just took a test in the only class that I had not yet yesterday, so we’ll see when I get all of those grades back how well it actually is going. I pretty much try to cram a week of school/homework/contract work/side projects into 3 days (Mon, Tues, Wed) and then have Thurs-Sun free to travel which definitely is taking its toll on my free time early in the week, but absolutely worth it. Ironically enough given that I am living in France and it should be the easiest class that I have ever taken at Tech, I expect that my Elementary French 1 test grade will be the lowest grade that I get back. I also am finally hearing back at all from some companies that I had applied to, which is nice since at least rejection letters are better than non-responses in my opinion – plus I have at least one exciting interview in my near future.

I also have finally moved away from eating the same 2 or 3 things for every meal here and even bought some individual items that you have to put together to make meals instead of open, heat, and eat type meals. I even started buying snack food and finding some hidden gems of French supermarkets that are pretty awesome.

I have a pretty funny story about last Wed night and my friend’s 21st birthday that is at least worth hearing, but again isn’t being written on here so you’ll have to ask me for it.

The United States

Since basically all of my contact with people in the US is being done through Facebook Messanger, I am spending a lot more time looking at my Facebook news feed than I ever would in the US. In light of the upcoming presidential election and ongoing primaries, everyone and their mother has to come out and vocalize to the Facebook world who they support and how all of the other candidates suck. If I see one more unintelligible rant or video about how Donald Trump is literally Hitler going to cause WWIII, or how Bernie Sanders is a geriatric socialist shitlord I will probably lose my mind. I’m not even going to share my political views on here, my personal blog, for a simple reason that can be summed up in one quote: “I changed my vote because of what that guy I went to high school with said on Facebook” –Literally no one ever. Obviously if you know me and my feelings on political efficacy that may not be the only reason, but FFS do these people really think that they are gaining anything from getting in the most petty comment flame wars with a bunch of other people who also have strong enough of opinions to share them on Facebook. /rant

I realize that none of that applies to my trip to Europe or specifically Munich, but I wanted to rant about it and at least now it was read by all 4 people who will probably see this.
