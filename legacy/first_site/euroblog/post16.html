Marseille

Hidden Gem of France

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Apr 18, 2016

Super short blog post because I don’t have time this week. James and I went to Marseille for the weekend and it was pretty incredible, I don’t know how the calanques here aren’t way more famous – they may have even topped the Alps for most scenic sights of my entire trip. We had planned to go sea kayaking Saturday morning but the outfitter scrapped it because it was too windy. Another group that had gone a few weeks before us had the same issue so it seems not too uncommon of an issue. We spent all day on Saturday hiking from The Calanques of Marseille all the way along the coast to the city of Cassis. I’m not going to go into too much detail on it because time, but you can read some randos blog about it here that I didn’t actually read but stumbled upon trying to find the length of our hike and it seemed to have some details and pics in it. I have done more than my fair share of hiking, granted all in the south eastern United States, but this was a truly unique experience. Everything about it was so different from what I am used to, and I have never felt compelled to take so many pictures while hiking. Eventually I stopped because I realized that they all probably looked the same in cell phone pics and it would have been hundreds of those same pics by the end of the day. It was definitely the most physically demanding hiking that I have ever done, but absolutely worth it as it topped the list of nicest scenery I have ever experienced as well. It was about 15 miles which isn’t that bad, but the terrain was intense to say the least so it felt like far more, and would have been much more manageable if we had brought more food and water. For more superlatives from the trip it might have been the hungriest that I have ever been but it made that burger stand in Cassis so so satisfying at the end. It also had me thinking about food a lot and there are a lot of random things that I miss. I’m going to make a list under extras somewhere next time that I get a chance with the things I am looking forward to about returning home, as well as a checklist of things I want to do in the month between my return and starting back at work in June.

Other than that spent some time on the beach. They were the best maintained beaches that I have seen in Europe – far less litter and broken glass than everywhere else. Didn’t really do much sightseeing stuff, though from what I did see I really liked the city. It had a very Spanish/Sicilian cultural blend feel to it which was very unexpected for France. It makes sense given the city’s history as a major port city in the Mediterranean likely with strong trade relations with Spain and Italy but it was still really cool.

Back in Metz I have a showcase thing for this CS class mobile app thing that I have been working on all semester tomorrow. I have a test on Wed and am leaving for London either Wed night or Thurs so it will be a very short week here. Finals start next week which is crazy. My eurail pass expires on Thursday which is sad – I never did take the time to visit Luxembourg or some smaller German cities as a day trip so I guess that will have to wait for next time. I have no food left in my apartment but also hate to buy groceries with such little time left here, esp when I am about to leave for London for a few days and perishables and whatever.
