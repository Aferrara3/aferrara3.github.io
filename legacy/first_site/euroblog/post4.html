Cologne + Amsterdam


Posted by Alexander Ferrara Jan 26, 2016

This weekend was pretty packed with travelling, seeing multiple cities in one weekend. We elected to leave on Friday morning rather than after class on Thursday as the Eurail pass is really good for 90 days rather than 3 months, so waiting the additional day will cover one way of an additional weekend of travel near the end of the semester. We left on one of the earliest trains out on Friday morning en route to Cologne, Germany. After a myriad of delays from delayed trains, broken down trains, and the ensuing missed connections we arrived in Cologne probably around noon on Friday so still had ample daylight hours to explore.

There aren’t a ton of pictures here, but the cathedral there was quite possibly the most impressive one that I have seen yet – right up there with Sacre Coeur but of a very different Gothic style. The people here (and in Amsterdam and Luxembourg) all speak English very well which was pretty nice. It seems like France is the only place so far that I have been where it isn’t expected for everyone to also speak it, so that was refreshing this whole weekend. The people here were great, all incredibly friendly. We were a group of about 17 GTL students and we took a free walking tour with this awesome tour guide who you can see in one of the pics on the Google Drive. He was so enthusiastic about his city and their stories for everything, it really made for an awesome experience. Afterwards we went out for a drink with him and even invited him to our existing dinner plans. This worked out better than expected because after dinner he even showed us around the district with much more student nightlife and hung out all night. I had planned to stay with a couchsurfing group, but for a number of reasons that plan fell apart at the last minute for me. You’ll have to ask for the details on that, but at least I had an adventure wandering around a foreign city in the rain at 2:30am trying to find a friend’s Airbnb floor. As a note on that, Airbnb is great and deserves tons of credit for the ease and comfort of our traveling so far.

The next day we headed over to Amsterdam, arriving there around 1pm. We wandered around a bit and grabbed lunch before another walking tour. Unfortunately this one was way way less good. Our tour guide was kinda creepy and just not good, especially as compared to our experience in Cologne, so we ended up ducking out of that and spending our remaining daylight hours more self-guided. The stores and restaurants here all had a very Americanized feel to them, many sporting the same exact brands for that matter. The small experience that I had with Luxembourg was actually very similar. At night I went to the Heineken Experience which was pretty awesome and I highly recommend for anyone who even finds themselves there. Other than that, some of the things and places that Amsterdam is most known for were definitely interesting to see how it all operates with the culture there and I don’t really have much else to add to that. One short story – it was kind of funny seeing a guy who was like in his 70s come out of one of the red rooms, especially juxtaposed with a guy who was probably <20 come out right next door at the same time.

The next morning we headed back towards Metz, with a 2 hour connection in Brussels – could have been made 5 if we wanted to stay there longer. We got off at the midi train station not realizing where it was and after hearing about how beautiful Brussels was we were all quite surprised. The graffiti per square foot is probably up there on the list of highest in the world. It was clearly an impoverished area, so we walked a little over 30 minutes to the more central part of Brussels which did seem much nicer, cleaner and safer but at this point were pretty tired of traveling for the weekend and elected to head back to Metz. Based on what I have seen online it is probably worth heading back to see the nicer parts of town at some point before heading back stateside.

As aside on McDonald’s

On one of the connections on our trip to Cologne we only had a few minutes but stopped in the McDonalds at the German train station. The menu there was once again completely different, featuring some pretty gourmet looking burgers for McDonald’s and CURLY FRIES. Didn’t have time to get anything there, so Germany did not get added to my list of countries where I have eaten McDonald’s – yet. As for the countries where I have, that list is growing…

• Paris, France

• Amsterdam, Netherlands – Only place I have seen a dollar menu and their McChicken has some bad ass sweet chili sauce on it: 10/10 will probably have again. I didn’t even really feel bad about this one because in Germany you have German food, France you have French food, but what the hell is Netherlandian food anyways.

• Luxembourg, Luxembourg – I had this while waiting on another train connection. It was the most expensive but best McDonald’s food I have ever had. Put on a nice plate and it could have come from a real restaurant + curly fries so that should speak for itself.

Regarding Picture

As usual the pictures are all posted in the google drive here: https://goo.gl/6Nc2Gw. There really aren’t a ton this weekend because there wasn’t a ton of "wow look at this recognizable monument xyz whatever" but there was tons of cool history and culture in both of the places that the pictures fail to capture. Oh also in Amsterdam you aren’t allowed to take pictures in lots of places and the places that you are still felt kind of sketchy like someone might take issue with it because of what ends up in the background etc.

Closing Remarks

Both Cologne and Amsterdam were awesome. I really wish that I had had more time in each, and may very well go back to either or both at some point this semester to see all that they have to offer. I also had some notes on my phone to mention that I just looked at and don’t want to go back and see where they fit in, so you just get them here: The German countryside is absolutely beautiful! Completely unrelated to that, nowhere here took anything other than cash. I didn’t bring much and had to turn my friends into walking ATMs and just Venmo them for cash because plastic wasn’t good for much in either of those cities short of the big chain places like McDonald’s. Lastly, I have pretty much just given up on my gps logger because it sucks everywhere other than the US and kind of France. I really like the maps though and Google location history while it sometimes has some erroneous data while on trains has been coming through pretty clutch.
