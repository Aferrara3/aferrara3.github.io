Metz

Some generic subheading

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Jan 13, 2016

Enjoy the astronaut again.

The past 2 days I have gotten around a bit more in Metz.

Yesterday I went grocery shopping for the first time and was a bit shocked at how different certain things were. Prices for any meat products were super inflated, as well as most condiments and things of that nature, while breads and pasta were super cheap, so I see myself becoming a [malnurished] vegetarian this semester. The hardest thing for me to buy however was actually shower products. Unlike many products, I didn't recognize any of the brands, and not being able to understand what any of the bottles said other than shower made things difficult.

I also purchased my first bus pass yesterday and ventured into Metz to see what the bars here are like. Ended up spending the entire night at one place that came highly recommended by a friend who previously studied here - whose name we even dropped to the bro bartender Pierre followed by 40 euros worth of shot racks for free! The bar was followed by a small adventure in Metz which is reflected in the map below. Unfortunately, all of my exploring in Metz again this evening is not reflected, as I did not have my gps logger on me. Oops. Having never really relied on buses, or public transit at all, decoding the bus maps and getting around has been an interesting adventure in and of itself.

After classes today I went downtown to purchase a new international SIM card. I'll post my new French number on the contact page, and if I call you from it it is most likely free (carrier dependent). Also as of whenever I get to it tomorrow you will be able to text my normal US number for free. A few of us then wandered completely aimlessly around the city for a couple more hours. We stopped and had dinner at a very quaint little restaurant downtown. Nothing fancy by any means, but it was probably smaller than my freshman dorm and the food was really good so it was neat. Most of the French people we have met (mostly shop employees and such) have been extremely friendly towards us, and seem overall very positive about Americans. Syllabus week is always the best one of every semester, so classes are chill so far. Tomorrow I only have one class in the morning and then will probably do some more general needs shopping before going downtown again tomorrow night for some bars that have karaoke on Thursdays, so that should be interesting.

I took a couple of pics that will be on the google drive, and also updated the overall trip map, though granted still not much to see there.

Once again I am not going to proofread this, so good luck.

More pics incoming on the shared google drive soon: https://goo.gl/6Nc2Gw
