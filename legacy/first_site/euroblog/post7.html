Italia Spring(?) Break

Beautiful country, less than beautiful weather

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Feb 25, 2016

Not sure if this would be better organized by day or city, so I’m going to go with a weird mix and hope that I don’t hate myself for it half way through. Obviously I don’t remember exactly what happened where and all of the details, so I’m going off of notes that I had made in my phone and will probably aim for the cliffnotes version to avoid writing a novel here.

Thursday : Metz -> Milan

Being the semi-responsible student that I am, I elected to independent study my stat class on Thursday so that I could leave earlier for my trip so as to add an additional day in order to see Milan. I was about to make some guesses about when I left and how long it took, but Google location history being awesome again means that I don’t have to. Philip and I left around 7am on the 11ish hour trip putting us in Milan around 6pm. On the way there we stopped in Strasbourg which seemed really nice, would definitely like to hit there up again. There is a pic of the train station in my photos so you can see it. We grabbed McDonald’s there because needed a quick lunch and it was pretty funny because when you connected to their wifi it took you to their website to buy McDonald’s merchandise because everyone needs that. I have a lot more comments about the McDonald’s here and throughout Italy, but I will be cutting all of that out and sharing that somewhere else as I feel that my blog posts are already oversaturated with information and stories about McDonald’s.

Had a stop in Switzerland where I went into a convenience store to find that a 6 pack of Corona was 16 euros. Between that and their 20 euro McMenu, I don’t know how anyone can afford to live here. Also while passing through Switzerland I was asleep on the train and the ticket lady woke me up to check my ticket, yet again, (and she was a total bitch about it) but I can’t complain because otherwise I would have missed one of the most beautiful scenic sites that I have witnessed I Europe. Not to discredit how beautiful the tops of the Alps were, but I looked out of the window to see a snow capped mountain surrounded by a lake, backed by a bright blue sky, just barely touching the clouds, with the greenest fields all around and a single sailboat in the water. New definition of lake-house. I dropped a pin where it was (Near Lucern) and will have to go see if I can find any good pics online because the crap that I took through the train windows were pathetic.

Anyways, Italy. Arrived in Milan around 6 which didn’t leave tons of time to see much as everything closes so early in Europe and there wasn’t a great night life here surprisingly. Walked around a mall though that was literally only the most prestigious brands and saw the Cathedral from outside that night. The ground outside of the Cathedral was covered in confetti which didn’t seem that weird since Carnival had been going on across Europe the week before; until the next day when the carts showed up selling more confetti and the ground which had been cleaned overnight quickly returned to a state where you couldn’t actually see the ground.

My only complaint about Milan was from dinner that night. I got an English menu and ordered the pizza that was topped with “Frankfurter and fried potatoes”. You can see a picture of what I received on the google drive and suffice it to say that it was not what I was expecting haha – one cut up hotdog and a mountain of fries on some pizza crust. It still had potential to taste good, but as my first pizza in Italy it was kind of a letdown. Fortunately I spent a week pretty much eating only pizza so I had a lot more to try. They also charge “cuperto” here which ended up being really common in Italy. It is a “placesetting fee” so basically you can just add 2-3 euros to everything for the right to sit at their table, sometimes with an additional gratuity percentage also added on. It really isn’t that much but hidden fees in general drive me crazy.

Friday : Milan -> Venice

Milan lived up to pretty much every expectation that I had – probably the most upscale place in the world. I mentioned it before, but literally it is a city centered around shopping with only the best designer brands everywhere. Granted I only did a little bit of actual shopping there myself, I actually enjoyed walking around the shopping district here. I was solo all during the day here which I have surprised myself to enjoy since I normally strongly prefer the company of others. In the morning I had gotten up super early since I only had 1 real day there and found that literally everything was closed. Most attraction type places had rolling openings from 10-11 which seemed really late to me, but since I was up I explored a really nice park. It was nice being in the middle of a big city but early enough that everything was so quiet and peaceful – the only other people that I saw in the park were a bunch of Asian people doing some yoga-ish looking stuff in very scenic locations.

I spent most of the day walking around shopping areas, but there are a good number of museums here as well. The pass for most of them was really cheap as well, but I didn’t really have enough time to explore them all so I passed. One thing that I really wanted to do here as well was see The Last Supper. I went to where it is kept though and the line without a reservation was suuuper long and there is no guarantee that I would even get in that day so I reluctantly gave up on that as well. That’s pretty much it for Milan, the Cathedral is pretty sweet and the entry ticket came with access to a religious museum as well which was pretty neat.

Got to Venice that night, but I’ll just talk about Venice in the next section.

Saturday : Venice

Venice was definitely one of my favorite cities on this trip. It is divided up kind of in to districts with completely different environments and atmospheres. Our Airbnb was gorgeous and in a really cool area with a lot of small restaurants and an outdoor market during the day. The layout of the city is awesome, with tons of tight winding roads packed closely together making it very easy to get lost, but fun to travel around in. You aren’t constantly walking along and across canals as well and the water there is the most beautiful teal color that you have ever seen. I did learn from a local though that night that the water is apparently really toxic and not safe to come in contact with because of pollution and runoff and stuff but it looked so nice. One of my favorite things about Venice is that there are no cars or other automobiles like scooters and stuff. Like at all. It is somewhat subtle until you notice it, but having boats as the main transportation in the walking city was really cool and there isn’t all of the normal city noise from traffic.

We had a pretty big group of 8ish stay together all day and had a blast. I am really happy with the travel group – there was a ton of great personality and it made walking around all day an actually really fun experience. Most of the sightseeing points of interest are really close together and stuff that you mostly just see from the outside, so we hit most of trip advisor/google things to do card places by noon. Most of the day was just spent wandering around the streets and canals of Venice, but I had a hell of a time doing it. At one point we were walking towards our Airbnb but Jimmy pointed out a small pink arrow painted on the wall (graffiti in Europe is really bad everywhere) so obviously we had to follow it. Then we found another one. And another one. We kept following this trail of arrows for way longer than we probably should have. At one point we found a door with one of the arrows pointing to the doorbell which we rang but no one answered. The signs on the door were in Russian and it was pretty sketch, so I like to believe that this was the intended final destination. Eventually the arrows are contradicting each other and leading in circles so we abandoned the adventure, but it was really fun while it lasted.

I really liked the way that the nightlife near the university bars worked here. It was like a big square of bars and then everyone gets drinks and hangs out standing around in the square. I especially like this as compared to most of the places that I have been in Europe where it seems like most people get dinner at bars and then post up at tables all night. All in all I really liked Venice.

Sunday : Ferrara -> Bologna -> Florence

I got up early and left for Ferrara since no one else really had reason to want to go there, and it was awesome. It was pouring rain the entire time that I was there but I really liked Ferrara, not only because my name is on everything, but because it was like a hidden gem in Italy. There was a bomb ass castle, a palace, and the cathedral had easily one of the nicest interiors that I have ever seen… and ya know, my name was on everything. I really wanted to tour the castle and it was super cheap for the ticket, but I really only had a few hours there so didn’t have the time to. Seriously awesome place though, definitely worth the detour.

Next I hit up Bologna which wouldn’t inherently be that cool or interesting were it not for the Kappa Sigma history there. Got to see some notable fraternity history landmarks though so it was pretty cool and the view from the towers of the city was one of the cooler cityscapes with all of the roads converging on your location.

Finally after those shorter stops we made it to Florence in the late afternoon. Our hostel was pretty weird, though right on the river. We did some city exploring that evening, which obviously everything was closed but there was some cool outside stuff to see and we made it fun, it was pretty chill. Did I mention that I went to Ferrara this day and my name was on everything?

Monday : Florence/Pisa

Early in the morning we headed to Pisa to try to see the leaning tower and take dumb selfies before the bad weather hit in hopes of getting some blue sky in the background. It was pretty cool, definitely leaning. We didn’t go to the top since Pisa isn’t all that exciting to see for 27 euros and the selfie game is so much stronger from the bottom anyways. We took lots of dumb pictures and then pretty much left back to Florence, 5 hours well spent. The gelato here was really good, so there’s that.

Back in Florence I just explored solo most of the day. Saw some stuff, but everything here was so expensive to get in to. Normally they have student discounts but in Italy it normally was only for EU students and explicitly did not allow ISIC students, so that was dumb. There is some cool stuff in Florence for sure, but the rain really detracted from this city a good bit.

Tuesday : Florence -> Naples

Florence is obsessed with David, and his genitals. 80% of the souvenirs here are of David’s junk and there are multiple monuments that have smaller recreations of the exact David statue. So naturally I wanted to go see David, but when I got there super early the line was already wrapped around the building. I elected to get headed to Naples since it was kind of far, but 1 person in the group did stay and it basically became his entire to wait and see David.

It was pretty far to Naples and once we got checked in to the hostel it was already like 2:00 and the sun set was way too early everywhere on this trip. First impression though: wow. This city was gorgeous. The city is built all along this mountainside around a cove-ish thing, it was great. 3 friends and I rented a paddle boat and took it out on the Mediterranean Sea (technically Tyrrhenian Sea but close enough) with a prime view of the city and Mt Vesuvius. That was a really cool experience, but the waters got pretty rough for a paddle boat and we had to bring it back in. We walked along these big rocks that make up the coast as the sun set and the city lights all came on, it was incredible.

Oh the food in Naples was also both the best and cheapest that I had anywhere in Italy, hands down.

The only time that I made it into more of downtown Naples was that night to the bar areas where our hostel check-in guy had recommended the best pizza places in the city. We grabbed some late night fried pizza, which is awesome, and ended the night chilling with a few bottles of wine down on the rocks by the coast again. It was chill, and the group that we had for it was a ton of fun.

Wednesday : Vesuvius/Pompeii -> Rome

For the uninitiated, Naples is right next to Mt Vesuvius and Pompeii. You have to take these small regional trains between them which are more like Metro trains than anything and are packed like sardines. One other person in the group and I took a eurail train there instead which got you like 30 minutes away from the Vesuvius Express place we were using, but a nice morning jog* near the sea and a volcano never killed anyone… well maybe not anyone, but it wasn’t bad. Took a minibus van thing up as far as it can take you and then you walk the last 800m or whatever to the crater rim. Embarrassing as it may be, I think that most of us were hoping to see some lava and didn’t know much about the different types of volcanoes going in to it. You do get to walk around the rim though and could see some steam coming out of it. It was cool lava or not - I was still standing on the rim of an active volcano.

*Oh I forgot to mention that starting in Naples I was able to ditch the jeans and wear shorts for most of the rest of the week which was sick, especially now that I am back in Metz and it is snowing.

From there we headed to Pompeii after a short layover at the Herculaneum Ruins on a regional train. By the time that we got there we only had 1.5 hours to explore Pompeii and could definitely have used 3 or 4. I had no idea just how much area would be covered by these ruins. It is crazy to stand there and think that used to be a city full of people and elaborate buildings and now is just a mostly destroyed hollow shell due to the same volcano that I was standing on the rim of earlier that day. We left when it closed and the group ended up split in 2 groups, one of 6 and one of 3. This is where everything goes wrong.

Hour from Hell

We had been warned multiple times talking to locals about crime in Naples with pickpocketing and theft and whatever and for the most part had been careful. Before leaving for Pompeii we had checked all 9 of our bags at the Naples train station on one ticket. So 6 of us get there first and claim all of the bags before the place closes and each are carrying 1 or 2 bags. 2 of our 6 sit down to get food at a food court with 4 of the bags, which the other 4 of us go to get food somewhere else in the station, not thinking about what a target for theft 2 guys with 4 backpacks look like. Long story short, one guy distracted them talking about the wifi while someone else snatched the backpack of someone from the other group from behind. Moments later when it was noticed that it was gone it was too late. We got in touch with the guy whose backpack it was to let him know, which is when he informed us that they missed the train that we were on because Zach’s wallet was stolen. I’m using names instead of pronouns now because it will be easier to follow. They were on the regional train from Pompeii at a stop and one person made a diversion by banging on the outside of the train while another guy snatched it from his hand and jumped off as the doors were closing. Zach and Matt pulled the doors open to pursue while Sid handled their other stuff on the train. The guys jumped on the train tracks not expecting them to follow but they did. The thieves had a decent head start, but Zach and Matt are 2 physically fit 20 years olds with a purpose and were gaining on them. About a mile later the 2 guys slid down some hill and ducked into an alley behind a warehouse where the pursuit ended since a phone probably isn’t worth the unknown of what could be in the alley.

Cut to our group who is now in Rome waiting for our Airbnb host to show up and give us a key. My phone rings from Zach’s phone and when I answer I am greeted by an angry Italian man yelling at me. I have no idea and though the man on the phone says that he can speak Spanish, our native speaker couldn’t understand him in that language either. Fortunately our Airbnb host shows up at this time and we get him to translate. He tells us that they have Zach’s phone (it wasn’t actually a wallet that they had taken) and are demanding 150 euros to get it back. This all happens just as we are finally able to get in touch with the two guys who have been mia chasing the thieves and we relay this to them. They were at a police station and arrange to meet the guy with an undercover cop on a motorcycle with them, which would be pretty cool. Unfortunately they called back to set it up and the phone had been turned off, or more likely died as most all of ours had in Pompeii without our backpacks/portable batteries. They had also just missed the last bus and the 2 hour reservation cut off for the megabus, so the 2 of them unfortunately had to spend the night at the train station and take the first train out in the morning.

Back in Rome we went out and saw some of the sights of the city at night. The Colosseum was pretty sweet lit up, but I was more impressed with Trevi Founain at night. Probably just because I had no real expectations for it and it was way more impressive than I had been expecting.

Thursday : Rome

Spent the entire day sightseeing in Rome. Shame that we only had 1 day though, definitely a 3ish day city to really see all that it has to offer. I was regretting having booked the flights for Sicily in favor of staying in Rome, but man after going to Sicily it was absolutely the right call – but I’ll get to that. Spent the first halfish of the day not really in Rome at all, but in Vatican City. I guess that I am really uncultured or uneducated, but I thought that Vatican City was an island, and I wasn’t alone in that in my group. In a sense it is an island within a city, but not surrounded by water so TIL I guess.

Vatican City was kind of a pain to get inside of since it is surrounded by a giant wall, but once we found the entrance it was pretty beautiful inside. Over the course of this semester I have already seen many of the nicest churches on the planet, but I have to say for nicest interior, St Peter’s Basilica takes the cake – fitting for where it is. The outside leaves something to be desired but then you go inside and it is magnificent. After that we grabbed some tickets and went to see the Vatican museum and Sistine Chapel. It was pretty cool to see the famous painting that everyone knows from there, but it was definitely not at all like I was expecting. Not in a bad way – just different. You can’t take pictures inside to help explain but I’m sure that you can Google it and see what I mean. You could probably spend a day in this museum alone, but while it may not have been built in one, I was trying to see all of Rome in a day.

Went and saw all of the major landmarks again during the day. I pulled up some history about the Colosseum when we were inside which really helped paint the picture better of what it would have been like and I actually learned a lot about what used to go one there beyond what I was aware of, so that was cool. I found it really interesting so I will share a link, but I imagine it won’t for those of you who aren’t looking right at the places that it is talking about haha. http://goo.gl/29yruu

Went and saw some of the other big landmarks before it got dark like the Pantheon and the fountain again during the day. Given its history with throwing coins into it, I was wondering what happened with all of the coins and the answer that I found was pretty cool. Once a day they turn off the fountain for 1 hour and remove all of the coins – approx. 3000 euros/day, or $1.26 million annually which then goes to a Catholic charity organization that cleans it, sorts it, and uses it to fund food and social programs for the needy and oppressed in 200 countries. So that was all pretty neat. The Pantheon has a cool hole in the ceiling called the Oculus that lets natural light (or rain) inside which was weird. One cool thing about Rome was just the grandeur of every building. Almost every building had these huge extravagant facades that in any other location would have been points of interest, but here were just more buildings.

That’s about all that I have on Rome, definitely want to make it back here again someday to really see all that it has to offer.

Friday : Palermo (Mondello)

Ah, Sicily. The flight here was pretty short, but the airport is about an hour outside of the actual city of Palermo. I want you to go Google right now and search for “Palermo Beach”. Every picture is basically the same, and that is what convinced me to come here. It is actually Mondello Beach and it is even more beautiful in person. The mountains on each side and the gorgeous clear waters are truly a sight to see. We went in to the small beach town for supplies before hitting the sand and it was a refreshing change of pace. The beach town was pretty quaint and the locals were SO FRIENDLY. Seriously everyone that I met was so genuinely helpful and friendly, which was so refreshing after being in big cities basically this entire trip where everyone who talks to you is trying to scam you in some way. That is one thing that I am pretty tired of in most of these big cities and has really turned me off to talking to presumable locals on the streets – it always becomes them demanding money from you for some “service” that they provided you. Here people just saw us looking confused or heard us speaking English and would approach us wanting to talk or offering genuine assistance, it was great. Anyways, we grabbed our Coronas, obviously, and beach mini kegs and spent most of the day laying in the sand – not walking 16 miles like every other day. We did talk to some locals about how to climb one of the big mountains on the side of the beach and followed some instructions only to find unfortunately that it isn’t exactly possible. We even tried finding a way to scale this valley of trees that looked doable, but I really think that there was a reason that you didn’t see anyone else up there.

In total I think that I actually only spent like an hour in what is actually Palermo, but this was the most chill and relaxing days that I have had in a long time and it was on easily the most scenic beach that I have ever seen.

Saturday : Catania

We arrived pretty late on Friday night and really just walked around the bar areas a little bit to scope out the town. Jimmy and I got up super early on Saturday and walked straight to the beach – well we kind of got lost straightish. The beach here was aight, extremely littered though everywhere and pretty windy. Every time that the wind blew too it threw all of the sand on you like little needles. I found myself in a rain jacket wind breaker thing most of the day, but still sitting in the sand with the warm enough temperature, and a sick view of Mt Etna was more than a little nice. James and I also decided to walk the entire length of the beach down to some green area on google maps. Once we walked the almost hour there, we unfortunately found absolutely nothing. Instead of walking all the way back we decided to try our hand, with hitchhiking. It didn’t take too long for someone to stop, but he was pretty sketch. I had mixed feelings because it was an older guy, but he had to take off a padlock to let us into the windowless back area of his vehicle where he had a kinda gross looking bed for us to sit on, so safe to say that we passed on it. Another car had pulled up behind him and was honking at us to get in which was kind of aggressive seeming which made it a little unsettling but walking sucks so we got in. It was an older white man and a black man not too much older than us. They were friendly, but also asking some kind of strange questions in their limited English. The whole situation kind of had us on edge, but we randomly guessed at where we should get out and it happened to be PERFECTLY where the rest of our friends had posted up on the beach so it actually worked out great – in fact I have actually now tried hitchhiking again here in Metz after the buses and taxis stop running and had another positive experience. About 20 minutes later the younger guy from the car walks up to us on the beach. We didn’t immediately recognize him but he knew our names. James had forgotten his Nalgene in the car and they came back and ran out onto the beach to give it to us. That really said something to me about the people here, as you would not have seen that kind of awesomeness from the people in most of the other places we visited.

The night life here was probably my favorite of the trip. Similar to that of the university bars in Venice, but larger scale and a larger variety of people. I really like this style because it makes it so easy just to meet locals and have some cultural exchange that you just don’t really get meeting random people back home who all come from similar areas and backgrounds.

Sunday : Fly Back

Sunday morning we flew back to Paris and made our way back to Metz. Had a couple of hours layover in Paris where I did a little bit of wandering and had some really good food, but the weather wasn’t cooperating so I spent a good bit of it just waiting at the train station. And that pretty much ends my trip. It was very tiring the sheer amount of activity and traveling, but absolutely worth it – awesome experience and tons of fun memories.

Random Other Stuff That I Left Out


Selfie Sticks

We always make fun of all of the tourists walking around with their selfie sticks taking cringeworthy selfies… until this trip. It started in Venice when the first couple of people bought them haggling with street vendors that are everywhere. After that everyone else slowly succumbed to the lure of the selfie sticks. We saw how convenient they actually were and at east secretly kind of wanted one. By the end of the week I believe that we had 7 of them among our group of 9, with Jimmy and I as the only holdouts. The street vendors also sold the same laser pointers with some disco lens and I did buy one of those however. When you’ve been drinking and some guy shows you a pen that can make the front of a cathedral into a light show canvas it is kind of hard to say no, and haggling with them is too much fun. I also bought this cool basket thing from one in Rome – same with taxi drivers and stuff here they all could be haggled to go so low.

Walking

Fun stat that I forgot to include anywhere: I walked about 150 miles during the trip based on friends’ fitbits and google location history.

Language

I guess I just had never heard real Italian, but I thought that it was a lot like English. As I learned it is much closer to Spanish. So right now I live in France, am taking a French class, and still can’t communicate with anyone here, but in Italy we could at least get by using Spanish. So go figure that would come in handy for me more than the little bit of French that I am learning while living in France for the semester.
