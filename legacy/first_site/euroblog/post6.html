Budapest


Posted by Alexander Ferrara Feb 8, 2016

Not a lot of free time this week, so this one will probably be shorter than recent posts.

This weekend I went to Budapest and it was absolutely awesome. Previously I had said that Paris was probably my favorite city, so it seems fitting how much I enjoyed what I was told is called “Paris of the East”. I took an overnight train there Thurs night - Friday morning so had 2 full days to explore the city and was completely occupied with things to do every minute. There were even some things that I would have liked to do but didn’t find time to just because of the sheer number of points of interest that I wanted to visit. The city is laid out wonderfully, with Budda being a more historic district with lots of cool history attractions and Pest being more modernized and touristy with the most incredible nightlife that I have seen so far in Europe.

As a side note to follow the chronology of the pictures, the 2 from McDonalds were at our layover in Switzerland where many of the meals were upwards of $20 and they had, I shit you not, a red carpet leading into the place. The first couple of pics in Budapest are from right after we got off of the train there, including an Aldi because for some reason it interests me that those seem to be by far the most common grocery store in Europe and a giant McDonalds to rule them all. Then there are some pics of the Airbnb because it was soo nice for an incredible $8/night each without even sleeping on floors. Everything here was so cheap.

The basilica pictured was really cool because it was so incredibly different from every other church that I have seen while in Europe. The amount of natural lighting inside and general architecture and decoration inside was really different and cool. Also there is a picture there of Saint Stephen’s mummified right hand so that’s neat in a creepy way. The house of Terror is shown from the outside - a museum displaying a bunch of stuff about the history there and the horrors of dictatorships and political evils, but unfortunately they did not allow any pictures inside. This was actually somewhat of a recurring theme here with places not allowing pics, but I got to see them so sorry folks.

I don’t have a ton of time so am going to skip talking about a lot of the other landmarks pictured. You can google “Things to do in Budapest” and see the cards that are a lot of the same things that you’ll see in my pics, mine are just better. The views there were all gorgeous from all different sides of the city, seriously some of the most incredible sights of my life. I’ll pick a couple of random neat things to mention. We happened to be by the President’s estate at the right time so saw their equivalent of the changing of the guards which was pretty cool. I went on this tour of a cave that was made to be a hospital in WWII and then became a nuclear bunker in the Cold War during the Soviet occupation and now is a wax museum with tons of the original equipment still there (again no pics allowed). It was really cool and in the gift shop I almost bought an authentic gas mask from WWII/Cold War but thought that might be weird to go through airport security with. For $8 it was hard to pass up though because this country doesn’t understand money. Speaking of which, I had multi-thousand dollar bills all the time so that was fun and went to an all you can eat buffet of Hungarian food for like $7 so the prices here were definitely refreshing coming off of skiing in the Alps last weekend.

Oh another really unique church here was one that was built hidden in a cave. Inside was some of the most incredible wood work in the world and a cool atmosphere but for the nteenth time I couldn’t take pictures here. Watched the sunset from their Statue of Liberty with another sweet night view so that was cool. Nowhere near as nice as our Statue of Liberty though so America #1 NA.

I briefly mentioned the night life here. It. was. insane. Unlike anything I have ever seen before. Our Airbnb was right in the middle of it which worked out great. Bars with small facades would open up inside into these massive establishments with multiple floors and 10 very unique rooms, different types of bars, and basically concert venues within them. They were all PACKED with college age people too so that was cool. No other night life I have seen has really even held a candle to it in my opinion.

Lots of other stuff but not typing it now so maybe I’ll have some better stories about one of my favorite cities next time you talk to or see me. I am pretty done with these 15 hr each way trains now (esp on 30 min of sleep, oops), but have at least one more week of it with my 11 days through Italy kicking off this Thursday with my Spring(?) break.

Random other notes from phone that I didn’t include:

Swiss McDonalds on top of being super expensive was funny because they had the exact same sizes for drinks and fries but called them “mini, small and medium” since I guess there is a negative stigma with the gluttonous sounding “large”. This trip went unbelievably smoothly. Jimmy and I used maps and stuff to actually plan out where to go in a smart order instead of back and forth across the city and it made the trip so much more effective and organized which I liked a lot. German high speed trains are so much nicer than any other train system in Europe. I’ve been on them a few times the last couple of weekends and the rest of the continent needs to take a lesson from them - keep up Europe.
