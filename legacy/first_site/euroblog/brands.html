Branding & Globalization

Rankings

Before I get into the details of my thoughts on each company, let me start off by ranking what in my opinion are the most prevelant and globally recognized brands in the world - given my blog posts the answer might surprise you. Then I will go through each detailing why it falls where it does in reverse order because that makes it more interesting.

  1. Coca-Cola
  2. McDonald's
  3. H&M
  4. Starbucks
  5. Burger King
  6. Subway
  7. Swarovski
  8. UPS
  9. Dunkin Dounts

Dunkin Donuts

Apparently not only America runs on Dunkin. I see them pretty often in Germany and have seen a couple in passing in Switzerland and Spain. Definitely common enough to warrant making this list.

UPS

When I have bought stuff from overseas before I have had to use DHL or smaller courier services to have packages shipped, so maybe I just assumed that UPS wasn't a thing here. They may not always Love Logistics in english, but you still see the iconic brown trucks and uniforms all over the place. I don't think that I have been to many places where I didn't see any. Most interesting that I have seen were foot couriers with pushcarts of UPS packages.

Swarovski

To me this is a weirdly upscale/expensive place to be as common as it is here, but you can't walk through any shopping area without seeing at least one or two of these. As far as stores go it is definitely the second most common that I have seen to H&M.

Subway

Subway is everywhere. Honestly it is a tough call between Starbucks/BK/and Subway for which goes where as they are all incredibly common. I never actually had Subway here until last week and was so pleasantly surprised to find the first SODA FOUNTAIN that I have seen in Europe. I'm pretty tired of the no-refills expensive af drinks over here, especially with fast food, so that was refreshing. That may have only been because it was in Paris and appealing to tourists, and McDonald's matched this luxury at the Mannheim train station a couple of days later so I dind't let this cloud my rankings.

Burger King

While I am far from a BK fan, they do get their credit for being everywhere. Leagues away from the prevelance of McDonald's, but you can find them in and around a substantial number of train stations.

Starbucks

Starbucks has spread in the most unique way in my opinion. If you are looking for McDonald's you look for the golden arches. Want Burger King or Subway? You know the logo. In many cases the facade of Starbucks is based on the area that it is in. For instance it may have more subtle-looking stone signage in artsy districts and stuff. Their menu here isn't exactly the same and missing the things that I like so that's lame. Pretty common to see though - not often you will go to a train station or main tourist area without seeing one.

H&M

I didn't even know what it was for a while, but it is most common store here by far. I haven't even seen any dominating supermarket chains here (aside from Coop in Switzerland), and this is the only shopping that I really consistently see in every country that I go to which makes it pretty impressive to me.

McDonald's

If you read my blog posts you already know about this sick obsession that I have developed with McDonald's. It isn't because the food is spectacular, but more of a fascination with the incredible job that they have done at promoting their brand. The golden arches are without a doubt the most recognizable logo in the world, rivaled only by that of potentially Coca-Cola, but I will get to that later. Seriously in any of the big cities that I have been traveling to you can normally look around and within a 360 degree view see 1-4 McDonald's. The food (aside from Switzerland and sometimes France) is consistently priced - or the value menu items at least. In some places they have crazy creative and fun menus like the "Taste of America Menu" or "Big Tasty Menu". No matter where you are, you will find McDonald's. When it comes to stores or restaurants or services or whatever, McDonald's is hands down the most recognizable and consistent.

Coca-Cola

The surprise number one seed. Is the Coke logo the most recognizable? Arguably - but what really sets it above the rest is that no matter what store or what restaurant, including all of the ones in this list, you will find Coca-Cola. Over here it seems like there is even less competition in the soft drink market than there is in the US. I know that I haven't seen a single Pepsi product or foreign sounding brands, and everywhere that I go still sells soda. It slips out under the radar being more of a footnote to anywhere that you would find it, but it is so pervasive through through all different types of POS it is just absolutely incredible.

But really, where the hell can I find a Walmart?
