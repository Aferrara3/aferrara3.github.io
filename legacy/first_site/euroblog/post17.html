London

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Apr 26, 2016

This post has taken me far longer to get done than it should have. I had hours on a MegaBus to do it as well as time yesterday, but for some reason writing a blog post that I know will be long again is daunting this time, especially given how short I went last week. This weekend I was in London Friday and Saturday. I had played with some different options such as leaving Wed night instead of Thursday or coming back overnight Sunday instead of Sunday morning, but in the end greatly limited my time in England in favor of not missing classes. In hindsight this was definitely the wrong move as there ended up being so much more there that I wanted to do and see, but I just ran out of time. Anyways, I’m just going to run through my days I guess.

I left on Thursday afternoon on a train to Brussels to catch an overnight MegaBus to London. When I arrived there it was weird – my Eurail pass that has been the most important and valuable item each of us have had all semester (given how hard they are to replace we were told to basically treat them as $1100 bills) became a worthless sheet of paper. It was an awesome investment that though I questioned its worth at times will definitely be missed. I was traveling to London with 3 others and meeting a larger group of 9 there – including @Ben, a friend who is a GT student studying abroad in Madrid this semester. He and I had planned for months to meet a friend of ours that we had met on our mission trip to Jamaica last summer but due to some unforeseen circumstances that fell through very last minute. It would have been really cool the number of places that the three of us together would have represented together. I, a Georgian studying in France, would be in London with my friend from New York who is studying in Spain and our mutual Polish friend who we met in Jamica that is living in England – but alas it did not work out.

Shameless plug for that mission trip as well – I cannot speak highly enough about my experience there last year with the Missionaries of the Poor. The work that they are doing is incredible and it was such a regarding and formative experience working alongside them for a week with the people of Kingston. I intend to make the same trip this summer and if you would like to support it we have just started fundraising through gofundme here:. Alternatively if you don’t want 10% to go to gofundme’s cut, you can donate without any dumb transaction fees to me directly through Square, Paypal, Venmo, Google Wallet, Cash, Check, Bank Transfer, or pretty much any other way to transfer money electronically.

I have gone super tangential to my trip timeline, so anyways I got to Brussels Thursday night. Like my previous trip including Brussels I only had a few hours here, but this time I tried to use it more effectively and check some of the stereotypical Belgium/Brussels boxes. As you’ll see in my pictures I went to see the peeing boy statue. It was somewhat of a letdown – super tiny. The recreations that you see in 50% of US backyards are all way bigger than the real thing. If you are confused by the picture of 3 of us standing in front of it, it is part of a picture series that some of my friends are doing imitating statues. Here is a way better example of one but they have been taking probably a hundred of them across Europe in museums and stuff this semester. Other Belgium things – I checked off true Belgian waffles and it was incredible. So much better than any waffles I have ever bought out in the US, but then again I have never bought waffles covered in different kinds of chocolate and stacked with whipped cream in the US to be fair.

Our MegaBus was scheduled to pick us up 12:15, but didn’t end up getting there until 1am and then the boarding process was abysmal as compared to the Flixbuses that we watched board. This was the same thing on the way home too which was surprising because MegaBus is a big name and I would expect them to have their shit together to make this a smooth process by now. On the ride there the bus wasn’t too bad because it was really empty and we each had our own row of 2 seats. Where it did suck was that around maybe 3 am you have to stop and get off of the bus to go through passport control and customs, and then again around 3:30 to get off of the bus once it pulls onto the ferry. Crossing the channel we were on a ferry that was kind of like a small cruise ship inside, but with less fun stuff and more sitting and dining areas. Most people gave into sleeping on hard uncomfortable chairs pushed together, but a little bit of extra wandering allowed Plager and I to snag a 12’-ish long couch as someone got up which made sleeping there for a couple of hours so much more bearable. Then we got back on the bus to sit I traffic for hours to get into London. It was definitely some crappy disjoint sleep, but worth it to avoid paying for a hostel and stull getting there by 8am in my opinion.

I walked to my hostel which looked close on a map, but London is HUGE. It was about a 45 minute walk, almost entirely through a park though which was really nice. It was in the 40s and yet people were swimming in the lake there because British people are apparently crazy. It was supposed to warm up to 66 degrees and cloudy on Friday, but instead it stayed in the 40s and rained all day instead so it is safe to say that the shorts and short sleeve shirts that I packed didn’t make an appearance.

After a super quick stop at Buckingham Palace my first stop was meeting up with my tour guide for a 6 hour free walking tour. This basically took my entire day, but it definitely was well worth it. It may seem like a waste given that I couldn’t really visit many of the big attractions in depth, but you learn soo much about the history and the places that are super important but you might not even know existed. This is another one of those places like Berlin that I have so much information that I could share about places but I’m not going to type out here but go through the pics with me back in America and I will help share some interesting stories or history. I didn’t really do anything super interesting after that on Friday. I saw the Cambden area and got some cheap Chinese food. Speaking of food I ate way too much McDonalds in London (country 13 #cultured) – not because I wanted to, but because we were always rushed because so much to do and see and just not enough time so it was a series of small pit stops for food. We went out to Upper Street that night which was pretty chill but relatively uneventful. Our whole group was spread out at different hostels because it was really hard to find hostels with more than 1 or 2 beds available for some reason.

Talking about hostels makes for a nice transition into Saturday. My hostel was ass. Short rant about all of the things that sucked about my hostel: the bathrooms were really dirty, the toilets didn’t flush properly, the shower was tiny, they didn’t have a luggage room to store bags before/after check in/out, they didn’t have towel rentals so I had to use a shirt, they didn’t have 24 hr reception and I had to schedule a specific time for checkout earlier than 10:30am, and the free breakfast was a sham. On Saturday morning I went down for breakfast which was posted as being served starting at 7:15, but at 9:00 they told me that I had to wait until 9:30 and I didn’t want to be leaving to start my day until 10:00 so I never even got the included breakfast. On top of all of that it was really expensive for a shitty hostel and my room (2 be, so very small) was shared with an old man that I am pretty sure lived there based on his elaborate setup in the room.

The disappointing breakfast situation at my hostel led me to the supermarket across the street. I found that while everything in England is expensive as hell, groceries are the one super cheap thing that slips through. I bought some pastries and stuff for breakfast, and a half gallon of milk. I realize how random that detail is, but it was pretty funny the looks that I got when I was on the tube and busted out that much milk to drink from. Long story short, I ended up with a grocery bag with hella water bottles and this milk. I went through security at the Tower of London with it and when I went to open my bag the guy asked if I had packed a lunch and my nonchalant response of "nah just water and milk" had him bust out laughing when he looked in my bag.

Transportation in London was pretty good, but super expensive. I got an oyster card and hit the cap every day so had unlimited metro/bus after you spend 6.50 pounds ($9.50). That’s definitely more than most cities day passes, and the tube was unnecessarily complicated and spread out, but I really liked the bus system. I didn’t use it too much, but each ride was really expensive so I hit the cap easily and riding upstairs in double decker buses felt pretty London so I did it a few times. On a somewhat unrelated note, it felt super weird being around English people. It was a sentiment everyone seemed to share about feeling like the people around us still didn’t understand us, and being surprised at understanding small talk happening around us. I had a tendency not to read signs and things because I just subconsciously assumed that I wouldn’t be able to read them. I found myself talking to people slowly in simple sentences as though English wasn’t their first language. It sounds crazy, but it took a lot of focus to remember to make normal conversation with people there. It isn’t that way with GTL people or most other Americans, but something about being in a foreign place has conditioned us so strongly to assume the language barrier that wasn’t there in England. I really can’t explain well how foreign it felt being around my own language, you really just have to experience it. I wonder what the adjustment will be like being back in the US, or if it will just feel totally natural because home.

I digressed again from my day, but after getting breakfast I went to the Tower of London and met up with Ben and James. Most of the group went to Stonehenge, but having been there when younger I elected to pass since it took your entire day for the trip there and back. The Tower of London looks super underwhelming form outside is walls, but once inside it is huge and there is so much to see and do. We spent way more time there than expected and ended up leaving without even seeing it all just to make it to the next destination in time. I revisited Westminster so that Ben could see the classic tourist sights – Big Ben, Parliament, Buckingham Palace, etc and then headed to the British Museum. While I have really enjoyed some museums such as the Louvre, I am far from an art connoisseur. And that is exactly why I loved the British Museum. I didn’t see a single painting there, but instead I saw tons of statues, sculptures, some more modern exhibits that were super interesting and made you think a little, mummies, more foreign cultural items that you don’t normally see, and the list goes on and on. I really really enjoyed this museum, and it was like the one thing in Europe that was totally free. Oh free places – I also went inside Westminster Abbey for free right before a service started, so didn’t get to see the crypts or any of the stuff that the $30 ticket gets you, but at least got to see the inside and church part of it for free, so take that super overpriced ticket people. It was the only church in Europe that I have seen that was more than like $5 to go inside and with how many incredible cathedrals I have seen this semester I wasn’t about to pay that much but my tour guide came in clutch with the kind of almost attend a service tip to at least see inside.

I stayed until the museum closed at 5:30 and then went over to East London where James had been on an urban art walking tour, so he was able to show us some of the highlights. The whole area was super hipster in a really cool way. It was definitely parts of London that you would never see unless you took a weird alternative tour like that. Grabbed some fish and chips because London and had to so I checked off that box Saturday night. As for nightlife we went to a few different areas that were super populated and seemed like cool places, but as seems to be a recurring issue with big cities like this had difficulty finding good places (that we fit the dress code for) without a pub crawl – it was aight though, pretty chill. I made it back to go to bed around 3 because the night buses were stupid and got my 4.5 hours of sleep before I had to get up for my MegaBus back to Paris.

My MegaBus back kinda sucked. It was packed so I didn’t have much space to spread out to sleep or anything. I figured that it would be nice on the ferry during the day, but nope – on the way back the bus pulled onto a train and went through the chunnel so we didn’t even get to get off of the bus. Umm that’s about it. I took a train back from Paris to Metz, meaning that my first ever train was to Paris and my last train was back from Paris. I will honestly miss trains going back to cars everywhere I the US, I have really come to like public and mass transit. At the same time, I am really excited to drive my car again – idk why but I’ve been looking forward to it a lot lately.
