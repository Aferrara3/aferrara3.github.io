Berlin

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Mar 13, 2016

I am actually writing this entry on a train right now on my way back from Berlin. I brought my laptop to try to be productive on the trains, but am an idiot and didn’t download any of my homework and chrome didn’t keep cached copies when my computer was asleep. I want to finish as much stuff that I would normally have to do during the week though as early as possibly so that I can meet up with Becky and Jill as much as I can while they are here this week and I would probably do this anyways so it isn’t completely wasted time.

I feel like this has been said in other posts, but to reiterate – I LOVE GERMANY. Of all the places that I have traveled so far Germany consistently has the friendliest people and my favorite cultural experience. Combine how modernized everything seems here with all of the history and you have a pretty kick ass combo. Also it consistently seems to be one of if not the most Americanized countries which is always refreshing. It might have any of the most scenic places that I have been, but so much more to the experience and atmosphere that that still makes it better in my books.

Anyways I spent the weekend in Berlin and it was awesome. I’m going to jump back a bit though because I had some notes in my phone throughout the trip so it is easier just to go through those. I was travelling solo this weekend as the group I was going with kind of fell apart at the last minute in favor of going next weekend but I will be visiting with the fam that is here then so I stuck to the plan. Since I got here I had said that I wanted to try some travelling alone and honestly every time that I have spent a day or whatever traveling alone I have really enjoyed it. It is a very different experience – one that I wouldn’t expect to like as I am not the most introverted person, but it is nice and peaceful. I also like to visit cities a bit differently than the groups that I go with (not stopping constantly for long meals, moving with a purpose rather than leisurely, some planning etc) so it is nice not having to deal with other people’s habits and just see a city the way that I want to. I didn’t actually end up entirely solo though. I was with a group from GTL on the first few trains who were headed to Prague. As soon as we parted ways I ran into a couple more GTL people headed to Berlin and found out on the huge GTL groupme of a couple more guys in Berlin who I met up with for dinner one night and to go out with both nights. So all in all I spent both days sightseeing alone, but still ended up having a lot of company there.

Boom, day 1… or night one rather. I took a series of overnight trains because it was further than I had thought and I didn’t want to get there in the afternoon on Friday and only get 1 full day like I have been doing lately. I’ve mentioned this place on here before too, but I had a connection in Saarbruken for like an hour and absolutely love this city. It sucks that I have only ever been here in the evening (or middle of the night sleeping on a bench) and never during the day when the large shopping area around the train station and stuff are all open. I need to find a day to come up after class or something one day since it is only an hour from Metz – there are so many delicious looking and super cheap restaurants, tons of American brand stores, a Pizza Hutt buffet for $7, and the cheapest alcohol I have seen in Europe. Every time that I pass through there I hit up the same store in the train station and it is all so cheap and you can buy liter cans of beer and that is too awesome.

I failed to really sleep much at all on any of the trains overnight and arrived in Berlin at 6:30 am. I then quickly found out that nothing in Berlin opens until like 10-10:30 (and then closes very early at 6-6:30). In light of this I took my self-guided tour of a lot of the touristy sight-seeing stuff that you really just look at from the outside anyways. If anything it worked out pretty well because all of the places that are super crowded during the day were all really empty to see stuff. One of the first things that I made note of was the very German looking crossing signal people – only to later learn that the city is obsessed with them. Literally there are entire stores that just sell everything you can imagine with these crossing people on them. They are a guy (ample man (sp?)) with little hats and goofy looking strides that look super German. Fun fact though, they aren’t even of German origin but originated from the Soviet occupation and the people just took to them really well so they ended up spreading to the rest of Berlin after the wall fell. One last tangent before what I actually did in Berlin – in most of my experiences in Europe I don’t even have to say anything and people just know that I am American and default to speaking English, even without saying anything or at times trying my best to speak their native language. Here though I don’t know if I just give off German vibes but everyone kept talking to me in German and even trying to power through my confused and flustered facial expressions as I try to decode what they are communicating to me. Being alone probably contributed to that since they didn’t hear me or the people around me speaking English amongst ourselves like when travelling in a big group.

I dropped my bags at the hostel and went to the meet up location for a free walking tour that I had booked. I had done a couple of these in Europe already and really like the style of the. The logic is why pay for a tour that you don’t know will be good/worth it, but instead tip whatever you think it was worth at the end. This leads to super enthusiastic and friendly tour guides too who really seem passionate and interested in their cities and my tour guide this time was no exception. In the 4 hours of wandering I had gone past most of the same places that the tour went to, but had no idea just how much history I was walking through until the tour. My tour guide was had a masters in archeology and was pursuing a phd in classical history or something like that so she was super knowledge about all of the history of the region and it made for an incredible experience. A lot of the pictures that I share won’t look all that significant without the explanations of all of the crazy history that happened there, but I’m not going to get in to all of that on here. If interested definitely get me to go through the pics and explain the significance of all of the places to you because there are a ton of cool stories and history to share. For 1 example, you would never know from seeing a picture of an apartment complex that it is the site of Hitler’s bunker in Berlin where he spent his final months and eventually took his life – the parking lot of which where his body was burned. So if the pictures don’t make sense skip em and ask me later because I would love to explain them.

A couple of other neat things that happened around this time, I was wearing a GT coat and some rando German guy recognized it and asked if I actually went to Georgia Tech. He said that he had been to the US and actually went to Atlanta and saw our campus so that was neat. That is one other thing that is nice about being alone is that it makes you much more approachable to other solo people and also forces you to meet some other people just because it’s a lot easier to strike up conversation with locals for some reason when it is that or be a loner loser.

One thing that I have wondered about before is how the Germans view and learn about WWII. Like we learn about America and the allies as some super heroes crushing the evil axis textbook super villains – and as it turns out they pretty much see it the same way. There are a number of free museums and memorials and stuff that are pretty cool and they all paint this picture of the German people being almost held hostage by their own government in those times. So anyways yeah went to a couple of free history museums and stuff after the tour so that was cool. I also went to this one really cool chocolate store that is known for making huge intricate sculptures like Berlin landmarks and Fabrogeoi Eggs and stuff. I don’t know why that was significant enough to make it I here, but it made it in the note on my phone so I guess I liked it a lot at the time.

The company that had the walking tour I went on actually has a number of different tours that explore different cultures within the city, and some street art workshops and a bunch of other things that would take a week to experience them all. I ended up only doing their tourist highlights tour and pub crawl, but they had so many cool sounding options.

Saturday highlights speed round because this is getting long – East Side Gallery was pretty incredible. I’m not sure why exactly the ones that are super famous and recognizable are the famous ones, but it was cool seeing them in person. I’m not art interpreter, but there were a lot of cool pieces painted on the wall spanning a lot of different tones and messages. It is about a 0.8 mile section of wall that is left there for that, 2 sided so basically 1.6 miles of art, but one side is mostly just your standard graffiti so less interesting. I also ate dinner down there at a restaurant called “White Trash Fast Food” which was not what I expected at all. The pic preview that google had for it made it look like a little shack or street food stand but then there was a very large sit down restaurant behind it and some awesome food. There is some real marketing genius behind that place – as they have a nice restaurant, nice food, and near 0 cost for the appearance because when you are going for the random stuff thrown together in disarray look, you can actually just throw together random stuff that doesn’t match together and looks trashy and cheap and it builds the atmosphere. Actually a nicer restaurant though, they’ll get a positive Google review from me. The other place that I ate on Saturday was awesome too so I have to talk about it. When I was with the group going to Prague they told me that they have a ton of kebab in Europe and there is this one place in Berlin that is the best they have ever had. If you didn’t know, kebab here is not at all like you probably think of as kebab in the US with like food on skewers, or maybe I just live under a rock in the US. Kebab is super popular in every city here and super cheap, but I don’t think that I have ever had it after the first weekend when I had pizza with kebab meat and wasn’t a fan. I found the place and it was a tiny street stand with a line of 30-40 people waiting on it, so that’s when you know somewhere is good. It was like heaven in the form of kebab for like $3 – just absolutely incredible. I got another kebab on my way back from the pub crawl from somewhere else thinking that I had found my new favorite thing, but it really was just that one place that was so good.

So I spent most of the day walking around the city just seeing more stuff. Went for a walk in their huge park and that was nice. I like how every big city has a huge quiet park in it somewhere with nice touches of the local culture. That night though I went to the pub crawl through the same company I had taken the tour with. I went with two other GTL guys and it was pretty fun. Berlin has some really off the wall nightlife places, but we went to some pretty normal places that weren’t inherently that cool. What really made it was that we had 100 people from all over the world and everyone was awesome and super sociable. By the end of the night I felt like I actually knew a surprising number of the people there and they had some really great personalities – couldn’t have asked for a better group of people to spend the night hanging out with. One guy from Australia heard that we were from the US and immediately started talking about how much he loved the NFL. Apparently they really like American football there and will pack the bars for the playoffs and drink only Budweiser that one time of year. He was a longtime Panthers fan, so was very familiar with the Falcons as well so we got to talk NFL for a while which is enough to make me love someone right off the bat. The pub crawl started at “9:30” which meant 10:30 and we got to the last club around 3:30. This last one was really sick, but crazy clubs aren’t all that suited to my current lifestyle and drinks all night had been so expensive that my wallet was sore so I ducked out around 4am. According to my tour guide bars and clubs in Berlin normally pick up around midnight and have more of a “stay open until you leave” type mentality which means that they can be pretty bumpin until as late as 10am the next day so that’s pretty crazy.

I think that is about all that I have to say about the weekend. My hostel kind of sucked, their Google review won’t be so stellar. Pls ask for picture explanations there, even if you think you know what you are looking at I guarantee there is a ton of cool stuff I can add about most of the places that you see pics of I just don’t want to type it all out this isn’t supposed to be an informative history blog.

Last notes: I am so done with this cold weather bullshit. I checked the weather in Atlanta for the week and saw highs in the 80s and am so jealous looking at my highs in the low 40s. I should have gone to Australia and had 9 months of warmth this CY instead of an extended winter. I crossed the halfway point of my trip this week and am more and more realizing some things that I miss about America. Esp talking to people on the pub crawl about where we are from and cultural differences and stuff, America is pretty cool sometimes.
