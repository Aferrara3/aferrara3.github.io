Familiar Faces, Familiar Places

Posted by Alexander Ferrara Mar 21, 2016

Most of my blog posts cover Thurs/Fri-Sun, but for this one we are going to look at a full week Sun-Sun so strap in. First off, huge shoutouts to @Rebecca and @Jill who spent all week in Europe and came to visit me. I was planning on meeting them in Paris early last week, but then that school bullshit got in the way of things. Test + surprise group projects on Wed and normal weekly homework due on Thursday were all pretty looming, so I had a very busy couple of days. Normally I try to ram a week of schoolwork into 3 days, so trying to shrink that further to 1 or 2 was a nice little challenge. I got back last weekend probably around 9pm and made the mistake of sitting down on my bed when I got to my room. After spending 8-10 hours on trains all day I always find myself so unmotivated and ready to pass out when I get home. So after like an hour of being locked to my bed I somehow managed to make it a late night and power through a ton of my homework. I go to class on Monday and one of my professors drops the bomb on us that this big group video assignment thing is due in 2 days, which we thought that we had over another week to do so that sucked. Queue another late night for that and the decision that I did well enough on the last test without studying to rationalize not studying for this one either and my week was finally looking pretty free.

Becky and Jill drove to Metz on Tuesday and met me after class. We walked around Metz, where I learned that I am glad I ended up deciding not to spend an entire weekend ever seeing it, as there really isn’t much to see or do. Don’t get me wrong, Metz is great, just not a tourist hotspot for good reason. It was awesome having some new familiar faces around, especially family. On Wednesday I was able to meet up with them again for dinner after my late afternoon test, and then Rebecca stayed to go out to bars with my GTL friends and me, as it was Wednesday. Kind of funny aside, Google Now is great. Without any kind of input on my part, just from patterns in where I frequent, and stay at night and stuff like that it adjusted itself to notify me with things like “Time Home” being now to my apartment, or when to leave for class at GTL based on my calendar and such - all completely unprompted. So walking back from class Wed afternoon it lets me know how long my current walk is to Comedie Cafe, the bar downtown that I typically spend the most time at on Wed nights. I dismiss it and move on, but then again at 9:30ish when getting ready to go back downtown there Google is again, acknowledging the fact that it is about time to get to Comedie. It is so creepy and big brother-ish, and I absolutely love it.

On Thursday I skipped out on my only class so that I could leave bright and early with the fam for Munich. I haven’t really been in cars much since coming to Europe. Maybe a cab home, or an Uber in better cities, but overwhelmingly my transportation is all public transit buses, metros etc and trains. I had thought about it and how long drives back home now won’t seem like anything after a semester of casual 8-15 hr trips each way every weekend. Boy was I wrong. Being in a car still sucks. I don’t know why it is so different – maybe a bit more cramped, or paying more attention to where you are in your route, seeing other cars around you or whatever, or the monotony of the road, but it feels disproportionately longer than comparable train rides. Our first actual destination was Dachau concentration camp. It was pretty cool, if that is the right word to use for such a place. Museum there was aight, but what really gives you chills is walking through some of the rooms of the crematory area. Granted this wasn’t an extermination camp with body counts in league with Auschwitz, but 42,000 people is still a pretty damn high number when you are talking about human lives. So it had its interesting history and somber moments, definitely worth the visit.

Didn’t see a ton of Munich in general that night, or really either, because everything just closes so freaking early everywhere here. We got a 2 person hotel room, with the intention that I would just grab a hostel across the street since Rebecca and Jill would be using it for a longer period of time than I would. The hotel either messed up somewhere or just gave us a bigger room because it was all that was available, but we ended up with a super spacious hotel room with 4 beds in a great location, so that worked out way better than planned. Friday we visited the nearby Fuessen to see Neuschwanstein castle. The weather was as good as we could possibly have hoped for with some snow on the ground but bright blue skies overhead – made for a pretty picturesque setting for the castle. From the outside it was pretty impressive, but I had heard that the inside was somewhat underwhelming. Maybe it was just these low expectations, but I really liked the interior as well. It wasn’t at all what I would have expected and just different from anything else that I’ve really seen here so that got it some points. Did some night sight-seeing of Munich after all of that, it was pretty chill.

On Saturday morning Rebecca and I headed over to Zurich to meet a friend of mine from Atlanta who is working over in Germany for the semester with IBM. So shoutout to @Diane too for hanging in Switzerland this weekend… as well as being one of like four people not related to me who reads these rambling posts. Zurich was a really interesting city. Everywhere else that I have seen in Switzerland has been really naturally beautiful from all of the nature there, largely thanks to the Alps. Zurich is Switzerland’s largest city, but not really near any of that natural beauty from the mountains. There is a lake that the city is built around, but they do a really good job of making their own naturey feel to the city. For instance the lake is lined with parks with the greenest grass and tons of space to just lay out, and there are tons of other parks and gardens throughout the city. It was really nice weather during the day on Saturday and the lakefront parks were packed with people laying in the grass chilling, playing with sporting equipment, eating, studying, just whatever. It was so chill, great atmosphere – personally I could definitely spend a day or my free afternoons hanging out in some of these green spaces. There were tons of people just sleeping in the grass too both here and in the lawns around the botanical gardens which was pretty cool. When we were at the lake we even rented a pedalboat to go out on the water which was actually really nice. There were lots of other little boats and sailboats and stuff just hanging out in the area so it made for a nice scene.

We hit up the botanical gardens and stuff in the area too to see those which were really nice (and free which always makes things nicer). On that note, I once again went to Switzerland and ate almost entirely from the grocery store because all food here is hella expensive – like ridiculously so. The exception this time was that we ran across another Hooters late that night and needed it. Now that I am posting about a second one you might think that they are common here, but they really aren’t at all. There are a handful sprinkled across Europe, mainly Switzerland and getting some hometown tasting wings just had to happen eventually. Really all in all we spent most of the day hanging out in squares and parks and stuff, nothing super eventful but really nice and chill. At night we were advised to avoid bars and clubs by our Airbnb host because they are so expensive, but were directed to a street where everyone just hangs out and drinks in the streets. It was aight, no bourbon street for sure, but thank Europe for those open container laws, or lack thereof, which have made cheap nights out so much easier.

On Sunday we elected to get up really early to see some more of the city, but as usual everything is closed on Sundays. We tried to see some of the big churches there, but none allowed tourists on Sundays so that was dumb too. Also as a random note, the church bells here are absurd. You’ll be right between two churches and both of their bells will be ringing so loudly that you can’t hear your own thoughts for 15 minutes straight before each hour. Got a couple nice views of the city though and personally I don’t mind just walking around so it was still nice. We went to a “Coffee Museum” this morning too – a total misnomer. It was a museum in a house of some family who got rich off of the coffee industry, but they have a little exhibit that rotates through different things every couple of months. Right now it was all about Japanese Kimonos, so that was pretty different. It was nice and warm there and something unique and random to see, so I enjoyed it. I would definitely consider Zurich as somewhere nice to go for a relaxing quiet weekend getaway, not the place to go for constant bustling entertainment, but super chill as you might have guessed from the six times that word has appeared in this post already. I saw Rebecca off at the train station as she started her solo ride back to Munich. New travelers, they grow up so fast :’). Also watch for another blog post this week from Rebecca. Now not every single post on here will have the same “by Alexander Ferrara” on it. If any of my other loyal readers wants some post space for something cool in Europe hmu and we’ll see, demand is pretty high right now.

Also public transit in Zurich was kind of slow because trams, but the frequency and sheer number of stops everywhere made up for it. I think that I still prefer a nice thorough metro system, but definitely up there as one of the better setups that I have seen in Europe. As the highly regarded critic that I am about everything I’ll give it an 8/10.

Writing these posts on my trains home is so much more convenient, especially since I normally just straight up waste this time anyways. Props to me for doing it this way lately. I feel like I didn’t take a ton of pics this weekend since Rebecca was taking a bunch so I am going to get those from her and add them to the normal shared picture Google drive folder. I realize after writing this that it may sound like not a ton of familiar places, but I’ve already been to Munich even though I saw mostly new stuff there and RT that about Switzerland + Hooters, so the title still holds, let it happen.

I really can’t emphasize enough how awesome it was seeing family and friend here other than the same people that I am used to seeing every single day. 70 days into my trip now and it has gone by so fast, but it was definitely good timing for a little taste of home again. Next week Barcelona! One of my professors is a total asshat and scheduled a test for Thursday morning right before the long weekend, so all of my friends are flying into Barcelona Wed night for $55, but the cheapest I can find for Thursday is $175 and trains are all booked. I feeeel like my professor owes me $120 now.
